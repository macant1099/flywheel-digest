# PerkSpot

## What they do
PerkSpot is a Chicago-based employee discount platform built for mid-market and enterprise employers. An employer stands up a branded PerkSpot portal, PerkSpot loads the employee roster, and employees get access to a curated marketplace of negotiated offers across 25+ categories including apparel, electronics, travel, fitness, and groceries — with discounts up to ~60% off and cited average savings of $6,053 per employee per year. The product is a managed service: PerkSpot's internal merchant team sources, vets, and maintains the offers, while the employer essentially plugs in a benefit and promotes it internally.

PerkSpot raised $50M from Susquehanna Growth Equity in its first outside round and, in November 2025, acquired Perkopolis — Canada's largest employee perks marketplace (founded 1999, 5,000+ organizations, 2M+ members) — giving the combined company 20M+ members and a North America-wide footprint. The platform also extends through partnerships, including a built-in integration with lifestyle-benefits platform Compt.

## Business model
PerkSpot is free to employers: no per-seat fees, no cost increase as headcount grows. Members also pay nothing. PerkSpot monetizes on the merchant side — its merchant relations team negotiates discounts with national brands and local businesses, and those merchant economics (lead generation, redemption-based or trade-driven arrangements with the 1,000+ merchant partners) fund the platform. For HR buyers, the "free" positioning dramatically shortens the procurement cycle, which has helped PerkSpot scale to 1,800+ enterprise clients with a cited 99% retention rate.

## Strengths (as a LiveChannel competitor or reference)
- Zero-cost-to-employer model is a sharp go-to-market wedge that most LiveChannel-adjacent players cannot match directly.
- Offers are genuinely gated behind employer authentication — not public codes scraped from the web — which creates real vendor value.
- Rich admin analytics (login frequency, category usage, redemption volume, team savings) give HR a real ROI story.
- Scale and PE backing make it a durable competitor with enterprise-grade client success and implementation muscle.

## Weaknesses / gaps
- Product is narrowly tuned to W-2 employers; it does not natively serve associations, clubs, alumni groups, creator audiences, or consumer membership networks.
- "Exclusive" means gated from the open web — the same catalog is exposed across PerkSpot's employer base, so no single source has truly differentiated vendor deals.
- The source organization does not curate its own vendors; PerkSpot owns the catalog and the merchant relationships.
- Engagement data is aggregate ROI reporting, not raw first-party purchase and intent data that the source could use elsewhere.
- It is still a destination portal — employees leave their intranet to go to PerkSpot, rather than PerkSpot embedding into the employer's own communication channels.

## LiveChannel implication
Direct competitor whenever the source is an employer shopping for a perks benefit. PerkSpot's free-to-employer model is the most important competitive signal to internalize: LiveChannel needs a clear answer for why a source would choose an exclusivity-and-data proposition over a zero-cost managed-catalog one. The bigger opportunity is in source types PerkSpot doesn't serve — associations, clubs, alumni, creator communities — and in the data and embedded-placement gap: sources that want their own vendor deals inside their own channels, with first-party data, are not a PerkSpot buyer.
