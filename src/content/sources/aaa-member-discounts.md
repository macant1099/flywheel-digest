# AAA Member Discounts & Rewards

## How it works for a member
A AAA member authenticates with their membership number — either by showing the physical card at a partner point of sale, logging into AAA.com, or opening the AAA Mobile app. Discounts come in two flavors. First, instant savings: show the card at a Hertz counter, a Shell station, a participating hotel, or a restaurant in the network, and the discount is applied at checkout. Second, AAA Dollars: a cashback layer earned on eligible purchases made through the AAA Dollars Online Mall (Walmart, Target, Kohl's, Macy's, iTunes and ~80 other partners), through merchant gift cards bought at AAA, or at select in-network transactions. AAA Dollars accrue for 90 days, then can be redeemed for cash back, applied to a membership renewal, or used on AAA-sold products. Offers are surfaced via the discounts portal, push notifications in the app, branch staff, and email.

## Offer quality and exclusivity
The network is enormous — AAA markets "100,000+ partner locations," with some regional clubs citing closer to 120,000. Quality is a spectrum. At one end sit genuinely negotiated rates: Hertz AAA pricing, theme-park tickets priced below the gate, hotel rates through AAA Travel. At the other end sit partner offers that mirror publicly available promos. AAA publishes an average-savings figure of roughly $220/year per active user of Discounts & Rewards, and stacked with insurance and travel benefits, claims total member savings north of $1,000. That ~$220 is the benchmark number every "membership deals" program gets compared to.

## Operational model
AAA is a federation of regional clubs (Auto Club Group, AAA Club Alliance, AAA Northern California/Nevada/Utah, etc.) operating under the national AAA brand. Each club runs its own discounts program on top of national deals negotiated centrally. Vendor relationships are invite-only and curated, negotiated by in-house partnership teams — not an open marketplace. The program is tightly wired into the core membership CRM, the app, and the branch network, which means AAA owns the data and the member relationship end-to-end.

## What this tells us
Three takeaways. **(1) The "$220/year saved" claim is table stakes** — any member-deals program is implicitly benchmarked against AAA's number. **(2) Instant POS discount + cashback is the winning UX duo.** Members don't just want a coupon library; they want to feel savings at checkout and accumulate a balance. **(3) Exclusivity is a spectrum, not a binary.** AAA mixes truly negotiated rates with commodity offers and still keeps the "members-only" framing intact, because the brand carries the trust.

## LiveChannel implication
AAA is inspiration, not a direct competitor. It is what happens when a consumer membership org spends 100 years building its own in-house deals machine. The gap LiveChannel fills is for the other 99% of sources — clubs, associations, creator communities, employer groups — that will never have AAA's scale or staff. The lesson to steal is the structural one: instant-at-checkout discounting, a cashback layer tied to retention, and a credible annual-savings story the source can put on its renewal page.
