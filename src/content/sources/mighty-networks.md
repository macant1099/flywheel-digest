# Mighty Networks

## What the platform is for
Mighty Networks is a SaaS platform for creators, coaches, associations, and community operators to run paid communities under their own brand. The core unit of value is a "Space" — a feed-, chat-, event-, or course-driven area where authenticated members interact. Hosts sell access as monthly or annual memberships, one-time courses (often $500+), or ticketed events, and Mighty takes a 1–3% transaction fee on top of payment-processor fees. It is a platform for a host to sell their own content and gatherings to their own members, not a platform for third-party vendors to reach those members.

## Native deals / affiliate / commerce capability
Mighty has no native member-deals or vendor-offers surface. There is no built-in way for an outside brand to publish a discount, exclusive offer, or affiliate link targeted at members of a given community. Mighty's own Partner Program (30% lifetime commission) is a SaaS-referral program rewarding people who refer new hosts to Mighty — it does not surface offers to end members. Hosts who want to run affiliate mechanics for their own products can bolt on third-party tools like Rootabl or Rewardful, but those are single-product referral tools, not a multi-vendor deals marketplace. Any vendor "deal" inside a Mighty community today is an ad-hoc feed post.

## What it teaches us about member UX
Mighty is instructive in three ways. First, authenticated, email-verified membership with a single cross-network identity is the baseline — members expect one login to reach everywhere they belong. Second, mobile-first matters: Mighty reports more than 60% of activity on native iOS/Android apps, and its white-label branded-app tier exists precisely because members trust the host's brand, not the platform's. Third, the Spaces model shows that segmenting content by topic/tier is how members actually navigate — a deals experience that mimics this (a "Perks" space, pinned, branded) will feel native rather than bolted-on.

## Strengths to learn from
Authenticated identity, host-branded mobile surface, engagement-first feed design, and pricing transparency (flat monthly fees rather than opaque rev-share) are all patterns LiveChannel can borrow when positioning itself to sources.

## Gaps as a source-side platform
Mighty is not a deals platform and does not intend to be. There is no vendor-side onboarding, no offer schema, no attribution layer, no coupon entitlement, and no inbound brand marketplace. A community on Mighty that wants structured member deals would need an external system — which is exactly the gap LiveChannel fills.

## LiveChannel implication
Mighty Networks is adjacent, not competitive: it owns the "paid community" layer but leaves the "members-only deals" layer completely unserved. LiveChannel can integrate with or live alongside Mighty communities as the deals surface their hosts don't want to build. The UX lesson — authenticated members, branded mobile, topical spaces — directly informs how LiveChannel should feel inside any source.
