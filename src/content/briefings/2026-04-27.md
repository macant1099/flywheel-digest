# Flywheel Affiliate Daily

## The Lead

**Anthropic Built a Marketplace Where AI Agents Cut Deals — and the Results Should Make You Pay Attention**

Last Friday, Anthropic published results from "Project Deal," an internal experiment where AI agents represented both buyers and sellers in a classified marketplace, negotiating real transactions for real goods and real money. Sixty-nine employees were each given a $100 budget. Their AI agents struck 186 deals totaling over $4,000 in value — without the humans ever directly haggling.

The most provocative finding wasn't that it worked. It was the asymmetry: users represented by more advanced AI models got "objectively better outcomes," but the users on the losing end didn't realize they were worse off. Anthropic flagged the emergence of "agent quality gaps" — a world where the AI negotiating on your behalf matters as much as the product you're selling.

For affiliate vendors, this is the clearest preview yet of where commerce is headed. If AI agents start autonomously discovering, evaluating, and purchasing products through affiliate channels, the vendors whose product data, pricing feeds, and commission structures are machine-readable will win. The ones still optimizing for human eyeballs on a landing page will get outbid by an algorithm they never see.

Don't treat this as a curiosity. Treat it as a dress rehearsal for 2027's affiliate landscape.

## What's Moving

- **Meta's affiliate tools go live on Instagram Reels.** Creators can now tag up to 30 products per Reel with affiliate links, and tagged content flows into Meta's Partnership Ads Hub. Why it matters: the platform that owns the creator relationship now owns the attribution — and brands report TikTok Shop-like mechanics are arriving fast.

- **IAB Australia's 2026 State of the Nation report drops bullish numbers.** 42% of advertisers increased affiliate investment this year; two-thirds of publishers reported revenue growth; 90% expect affiliate importance to increase. Travel has overtaken retail as the top-performing publisher vertical. Why it matters: affiliate is no longer defending its seat at the table — it's claiming a bigger one.

- **Instagram formally re-enters affiliate commerce.** Creators can tag products directly in Reels and earn commissions on sales, while brands like Target have shifted away from standard creator commissions. Why it matters: the platform-native affiliate model is replacing ad-hoc influencer deals with trackable, performance-based partnerships.

- **Capgemini reports 38% of shoppers already trust AI agents for routine purchases.** Their research also shows AI-driven holiday traffic surged 693% year-over-year, with revenue per AI visit up 254%. Why it matters: the consumer behavior shift isn't coming — it's here, and affiliate programs need structured data to be discoverable by agents.

- **US influencer economy projected to hit $44 billion by end of 2026.** The Association of National Advertisers data is driving fresh calls for pay transparency across the creator-affiliate spectrum. Why it matters: more money flowing through affiliate-adjacent channels means more regulatory scrutiny and more pressure to professionalize program terms.

## Partner Landscape

**This Week: AI Shopping Agents as Affiliate Partners**

The traditional affiliate partner taxonomy — content sites, coupon aggregators, loyalty programs — is getting a new category forced onto it: autonomous AI shopping agents. Ulta Beauty just launched "Ulta AI," a Gemini-powered shopping assistant built with Google, while Sephora and Carrefour have deployed their own agentic commerce pilots in the past month.

These agents don't browse like humans. They consume structured product feeds, compare commission-weighted pricing, and route purchases through whichever affiliate link offers the best value for their user. For vendors, the implication is stark: if your affiliate program doesn't expose clean product data via API or structured feed, AI agents will route around you to a competitor who does.

The vendors winning early are the ones treating AI agents as a first-class affiliate partner type — with dedicated feeds, competitive commission tiers, and machine-readable program terms. This isn't a 2028 problem. Anthropic just proved agents can close deals today.

## The AI Angle

Anthropic's Project Deal and Ulta's Gemini-powered shopping assistant landed in the same week, bookending the agentic commerce story: agents can now both negotiate purchases autonomously and serve as branded shopping concierges. Meanwhile, OpenAI quietly scaled back its Instant Checkout feature, suggesting the winner in AI commerce may not be the LLM provider but the platform (Meta, Google, retailers) that controls the product catalog and affiliate rails.

## One Thing to Do Today

Audit your product feed for machine readability. Pull up your affiliate network's product catalog export and ask: could an AI agent parse this to find your best-selling item, its price, commission rate, and availability — without visiting your website? If the answer is no, that's your Q2 priority.
