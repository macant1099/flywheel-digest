# Flywheel Affiliate Daily

## The Lead

**AI Traffic Now Converts Better Than Humans — And It's Reshaping Affiliate Economics**

Adobe dropped a bombshell this week: AI-referred traffic to U.S. retail sites surged 393% in Q1 2026 compared to a year earlier. But the headline number isn't even the real story. The real story is that AI visitors now convert 42% better than traditional human shoppers — a complete reversal from March 2025, when AI traffic converted 38% *worse*.

Let that sink in. In twelve months, AI went from a curiosity that browsed but didn't buy to a conversion machine that outperforms your best organic customer. Revenue per visit from AI sources is now 37% higher than non-AI traffic, engagement rates run 12% above baseline, and AI visitors spend 48% more time on site. The era of "AI traffic doesn't monetize" is officially over.

For the affiliate industry, this is a structural inflection point. Affiliate programs already have the infrastructure that agentic commerce needs — tracked links, commission structures, attribution models, payment rails. The question is whether vendors are building their sites to be *discoverable* by AI agents, not just by human shoppers. Adobe's own audit found that roughly a quarter of retailer homepage content and a third of product page content can't be properly accessed by LLMs. That's revenue left on the table.

The vendors who move fastest to optimize product feeds, structured data, and catalog accessibility for AI agents will capture a disproportionate share of this rapidly growing channel. Every week you wait is a week your competitors are training the next generation of shopping agents to recommend them instead of you.

## What's Moving

- **Target scraps its commission-based Creator Program.** The retailer is replacing cash affiliate payouts with a gamified model that rewards creators with gift cards and Target products. Why it matters: when a top-10 U.S. retailer signals that commission-based creator affiliates aren't delivering enough ROI to justify cash payouts, it's a warning sign for programs with weak attribution. Vendors should audit whether their creator affiliate spend is genuinely driving incremental sales.

- **Levanta acquires Perch+ in affiliate network consolidation push.** The move signals a shift toward all-in-one aggregation platforms that own the entire journey from influencer discovery to payment processing. Why it matters: network consolidation benefits vendors who want fewer dashboards, but it also concentrates bargaining power. Diversify your network relationships before you lose leverage.

- **APMA rolls out new subnetwork ethics code.** The Affiliate Professional Management Association's new code of conduct requires publishers and networks to disclose specific traffic channels, targeting the gray area where subnetworks bundled opaque traffic sources. Why it matters: stricter transparency requirements are raising the barrier to entry for low-quality affiliates and creating space for merchants to confidently increase commission rates for high-performing partners.

- **Meta expands Facebook Affiliate Partnerships to eBay.** Following the initial launch with Amazon and Shopee, Meta now lets eBay listings be tagged directly in Facebook posts and Reels. Why it matters: the social-affiliate pipeline is getting shorter. Vendors listed on eBay should ensure their product data is optimized for in-feed tagging — this is discovery surface you don't have to pay for upfront.

- **FTC targets ad agency collusion on "brand safety" standards.** The Commission filed against WPP, Publicis, and Dentsu for coordinating brand-safety policies that demonetized disfavored viewpoints. Why it matters: while aimed at agency holding companies, the action signals that any coordinated industry practice limiting advertiser choice or obscuring fee structures is on the FTC's radar. Affiliate networks should review their own brand-safety and content policies for antitrust exposure.

## Partner Landscape

**Profile: AI Shopping Agents — The Affiliate Channel That Buys for Your Customer**

The fastest-growing affiliate partner type in 2026 isn't a person at all. AI shopping agents — from ChatGPT's product recommendations to Google's agentic commerce protocol to standalone tools like Perplexity Shopping — are driving measurable, converting traffic at scale for the first time.

What makes AI agents different from every other affiliate channel is the purchase intent density. A human browsing a deal site might be killing time. An AI agent is executing a specific task: "Find me the best waterproof hiking boots under $200." Every interaction is high-intent, and the conversion data now proves it. AI visitors browse more pages, spend more time evaluating, and buy at higher rates than organic search traffic.

The infrastructure is maturing quickly. Visa's Intelligent Commerce Connect, launched April 8, gives AI agents direct payment rails. Google's agentic commerce protocol, announced in January, has Walmart, Shopify, and 20+ partners backing it. McKinsey projects the agentic commerce channel will drive $3–5 trillion globally by 2030. For vendors, the immediate opportunity is making sure your product catalog, pricing, and availability data are structured in formats that AI agents can parse — because the agents that can't find you won't recommend you.

The open question is attribution. Today's AI shopping agents often strip or obscure referral data, making it hard for affiliate networks to track and commission the interaction. The vendors and networks that solve this attribution gap first will own the economics of the next decade of commerce.

## The AI Angle

Commercetools identified "zero-click commerce" as the defining retail trend of 2026 — purchases that happen without a consumer ever touching a website or search engine. With AI traffic up 393% and converting at record rates, the zero-click future is arriving faster than most affiliate programs are prepared for. Vendors that haven't started optimizing their product data for LLM consumption are building for a channel that's already shrinking (traditional search) while ignoring one that's exploding.

## One Thing to Do Today

Run Adobe's free AI Content Visibility Checker (or any LLM) against your top five product pages. Ask it to extract the product name, price, key features, and availability. If the results are wrong, incomplete, or garbled, your pages aren't AI-ready — and you're invisible to the fastest-growing traffic source in retail. Fix the structured data on those five pages today.
