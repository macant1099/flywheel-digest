## The Lead

**Instagram Just Changed Who Owns Your Creator Commerce Data**

Last Wednesday, Instagram Head Adam Mosseri announced that creators can now embed shoppable affiliate links directly inside Reels — no third-party redirect, no external tool required. Eligible creators tag products at upload, viewers tap to buy, and conversion data flows natively inside Meta. For a platform that has historically been a traffic driver rather than a transaction closer, this is a significant shift. Instagram is now building toward becoming a full-funnel commerce channel.

The feature itself sounds like a modest update. It isn't. For years, brands running creator affiliate programs have routed through intermediary platforms — tools that solved the link management problem but kept the attribution layer firmly in someone else's hands. Clicks, conversions, and commission data all lived inside infrastructure owned by those third parties. Brands could see their results, but they couldn't own the data that generated them. Meta's move brings all of that inside the app, giving brands direct access to which Reels drove purchases — without leaving the ecosystem.

The strategic implication is clear: the platforms themselves are muscling into the attribution stack. Meta, TikTok Shop, and YouTube have all made creator-native commerce moves in recent months. The message to the market is the same: your affiliate program doesn't need a third party if the platform can be the third party. For vendors, this changes the calculus on where creator programs should be anchored and which tools genuinely add value versus which ones are just middleware on borrowed time.

Brands with verified product catalogs already in Meta's commerce system gain a measurable head start as the feature scales through spring. Those who haven't completed Meta catalog setup are now behind — not in traffic terms, but in data terms. In 2026, attribution access is the competitive advantage. If you don't control the data layer, you're flying blind on your own program.

## What's Moving

- **Instagram Reels goes native affiliate** — Adam Mosseri confirmed shoppable affiliate product tags directly in Reels, with on-platform conversion tracking. Vendors should verify their Meta product catalog is active and accurate before creator traffic scales.

- **Levanta acquires Perch+** — In a sign of consolidation, Levanta — the leading affiliate and creator platform for e-commerce — acquired Perch+, one of the earliest Amazon-focused affiliate networks. The move expands Levanta's marketplace with hundreds of Amazon sellers and publishers. Watch for similar roll-ups as the affiliate tech stack matures.

- **TikTok Shop's top creators are demanding hybrid pay** — 2026 data shows the emerging standard for top TikTok Shop affiliates is a guaranteed flat fee ($100–$500 per video) *plus* 15–20% commission. Pure commission-only deals are losing high performers to better-funded competitors. Vendors competing on TikTok Shop need to revisit their creator compensation structures.

- **FTC expands disclosure rules to live streams and short-form video** — The FTC's 2026 updates explicitly cover new formats including Reels, live streams, and short-form video. Increased enforcement is targeting micro-influencers and AI/virtual endorsers specifically. Make sure your affiliate partner agreements include clear disclosure requirements for all content formats.

- **APMA publishes first independent attribution audit across UK affiliate networks** — The Affiliate and Partner Marketing Association released findings from an audit of attribution rules across ten affiliate networks. Cross-network attribution inconsistencies remain significant. If you run programs across multiple networks, your numbers may be less comparable than you think.

## Partner Landscape

**Comparison Shopping Engines (CSEs) — Under Pressure, Still Relevant**

Comparison shopping engines — Google Shopping, PriceGrabber, Shopzilla, Bizrate, and their vertical-specific cousins — have long occupied a critical position in the affiliate ecosystem. They aggregate product listings from hundreds of merchants, surface them to price-conscious shoppers, and drive high-intent traffic that converts at rates well above content or social affiliate sources. For vendors, they represent an underrated but high-ROI category of affiliate partner.

The model is simple: merchants submit product feeds, and CSEs surface them in response to search queries. Affiliate tracking runs through the product feed, meaning conversion attribution is relatively clean compared to influencer-driven traffic. Cookie durations tend to be short (often 24–48 hours), but because buyers arrive with strong purchase intent, conversion windows are similarly short. These are buyers who already know what they want.

What makes CSEs interesting right now is the pressure they're under. AI-powered product discovery — through ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, and Perplexity — is beginning to replicate what CSEs have done for decades, but conversationally. The smart CSEs are adapting: integrating AI-generated recommendations, building structured data partnerships with AI platforms, and pushing deeper into vertical niches where general-purpose AI agents are weaker (specialty electronics, outdoor gear, industrial products, pet health).

For vendors, the takeaway is this: CSEs are still worth maintaining — their traffic quality remains high — but they are no longer a set-and-forget channel. Product feed hygiene (accurate pricing, complete descriptions, strong images) is now a prerequisite not just for CSE performance but for AI agent discoverability. The same structured data that makes your products shine in a price comparison table is what makes them findable by AI shopping agents. These are no longer separate investments.

## The AI Angle

Visa's launch of **Intelligent Commerce Connect** last Wednesday is the week's most under-reported infrastructure story. The platform lets AI agents make payments across multiple card networks — not just Visa — removing a key friction point in agentic checkout flows. Alongside Accenture's recent investment in DaVinci Commerce, the payments and consulting giants are now formally betting on agentic commerce as a new commerce layer. The pipes for AI agents to discover, compare, and pay for products without human handoffs are being built in real time — and vendors whose product data isn't agent-readable are being architected out of the equation before the channel even matures.

**Search your brand name + "affiliate program" on Google and see what comes up.** You're looking for two things: (1) whether your own affiliate program page ranks, and (2) who else is already ranking for it — coupon sites, review blogs, unauthorized promoters.
