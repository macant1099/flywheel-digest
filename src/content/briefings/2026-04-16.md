# Flywheel Affiliate Daily

## The Lead

**The Social Feed Is Now a Storefront — And It's Not Reversing**

This week crystallized something that has been building for over a year: the social media feed is no longer a traffic channel. It is the transaction. Instagram relaunched its native affiliate program with shoppable links embedded directly into Reels, making its second attempt after sunsetting the first version in 2022 — and this time, the infrastructure is there to make it stick. Creators can now tag products, earn commissions, and complete sales without ever sending a viewer to a third-party site. Simultaneously, eBay and Meta announced a new affiliate partnership that allows creators to tag eBay products directly in Facebook posts and Reels with no link-in-bio required. Shoppers tap and purchase in-feed. The funnel has been compressed to essentially zero.

The numbers backing this shift are not speculative. US social commerce is projected to surpass $100 billion in 2026 for the first time, with more than half of all social network users also acting as buyers. Later, the influencer management platform, reported $2.9 billion in verified creator-driven commerce in Q1 2026 alone — and doubled its enterprise business year-over-year as Fortune 500 brands consolidate their creator programs on performance-measured platforms. On Affilae, influencer-initiated partnerships now represent 53% of all activity by financial weight. That isn't a trend anymore. That is the majority of the channel.

What this means for vendors is direct: if your affiliate program isn't structured to work with creators on Instagram and TikTok, you're not running a full affiliate program. You're running half of one. The platforms have built the checkout rails; the creators are already on them; the brands that haven't connected their product catalogs to Meta's commerce system and established commission structures for short-form video are ceding ground to competitors who have.

The harder question isn't whether to engage social affiliate channels — it's how to compete in them. The emerging standard on TikTok Shop is a hybrid compensation model: a guaranteed flat fee ($100–$500 per video for top performers) layered on top of a 15–20% commission. Pure commission-only deals are losing top creators to guaranteed-pay offers. Vendors pricing their social creator programs for 2021 cost structures will underperform.

## What's Moving

- **Levanta Acquires Perch+, Reaches 60,000+ Publishers** — Levanta (the leading affiliate platform for Amazon/Shopify/Walmart sellers) acquired Perch+, one of the earliest Amazon-focused affiliate networks, giving Perch+ sellers access to over 60,000 vetted publishers overnight. Levanta simultaneously added Paid Placements — flat-rate creator deals with affiliate-level performance tracking — across Amazon, Shopify, and Walmart. For vendors selling on Amazon, this consolidation matters: your affiliate program options are narrowing around a smaller set of larger, more capable platforms.

- **Visa Opens Its Network to AI Agents** — On April 8, Visa launched Intelligent Commerce Connect, enabling AI agents to initiate purchases, handle tokenization, enforce spend controls, and authenticate payments on behalf of users. This isn't a prototype — it's live in pilot with partners including AWS, Mesh, and Highnote. The payment rails for agent-initiated commerce now exist. Vendors not thinking about how AI agents will discover and purchase their products are watching infrastructure pass them by.

- **FTC Enforcement Reaches AI-Generated Affiliate Content** — The FTC issued its first warning letters under the Consumer Review Rule in January 2026, targeting fake reviews, incentivized testimonials, and undisclosed AI-generated endorsements. In 2026, any affiliate content produced with AI assistance requires a clear, conspicuous disclosure — or the vendor running the program faces civil exposure, not just the publisher. If your affiliate partners are using AI content tools, your program terms and publisher agreements may need updating.

- **Cookie Deprecation Is No Longer a Future Risk — It's Now** — Safari and Firefox block third-party cookies by default. Google retired its Privacy Sandbox in October 2025 but kept cookies in Chrome with user controls, meaning cross-browser attribution gaps are real. Leading platforms (Impact, PartnerStack, Refersion, Tapfiliate) have fully migrated to server-side/first-party tracking. Programs still relying on pixel-based tracking are losing attribution on a material percentage of conversions.

- **Attribution Standards Under Independent Scrutiny** — The Affiliate and Partner Marketing Association (APMA) published the first independent audit of attribution rules across ten UK affiliate networks, finding that "early funnel" affiliate contributions were credited in 80% of test scenarios. The APMA has flagged additional attribution projects for 2026. Standardization of attribution rules — long a hidden variable in affiliate program economics — is becoming more visible and contested. Vendors should understand how their network attributes multi-touch journeys.

## Partner Landscape

**Loyalty & Cashback Programs: The Quiet Powerhouse**

Loyalty and cashback sites rarely make headlines, but they are consistently among the highest-volume partners in affiliate programs across retail, travel, and financial services. Platforms like Rakuten (formerly Ebates), TopCashback, Honey (now part of PayPal), Swagbucks, and dozens of vertical-specific loyalty apps operate on a straightforward model: they share a portion of their affiliate commission with the consumer as cashback or rewards points, creating purchase incentive at the moment of decision.

For vendors, the appeal is clear: loyalty partners reach consumers who are ready to buy — they've navigated to a checkout page, they're actively comparing final costs, and cashback is their last-mile conversion trigger. Studies consistently show cashback shoppers have higher average order values than organic traffic and lower cart abandonment rates.

The 2026 evolution worth watching is the integration of AI into loyalty infrastructure. Wildfire Systems' platform now enables AI tools and shopping agents to earn affiliate commissions and pass savings back to users as cashback — effectively making AI agents the next generation of loyalty intermediary. As AI agents negotiate purchases on behalf of users, the cashback/rewards model is likely to migrate from browser extension to AI assistant. Vendors who have established clean API-accessible product data and competitive affiliate commissions will be better positioned to participate in this shift.

If you haven't audited which cashback and loyalty platforms are already sending you traffic, that's worth doing today. You may have active partners you haven't formally onboarded — and formalizing those relationships typically unlocks higher placement, promotional features, and better reporting.

## The AI Angle

Visa's launch of Intelligent Commerce Connect on April 8 removed the last major payment infrastructure argument against AI agent commerce: these agents can now securely initiate, tokenize, and complete real transactions. Combined with Stripe's Agentic Commerce Suite (built on the ACP protocol) and Shopify's multi-agent storefront distribution, the infrastructure stack for AI-driven purchasing is now effectively complete. The open question — who earns the commission when an AI agent recommends and completes a purchase — remains unresolved, but Wildfire Systems' RevenueEngine (which routes AI-generated product recommendations through 50,000+ affiliate merchant programs) is emerging as a practical answer. Vendors who have clean product feeds, competitive commissions, and server-side tracking are materially more likely to be monetizable within AI commerce infrastructure than those who don't.
