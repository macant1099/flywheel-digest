## Overview

LiveChannel is a connected commerce platform developed by Goodwell Technologies, Inc., based in Bellevue, Washington. Founded in 1999, the company has carved out a distinctive niche by bridging the gap between direct-to-consumer (DTC) and marketplace e-commerce models. LiveChannel's defining product is its role powering Costco Next (CostcoNext.com), Costco Wholesale's innovative member-exclusive online marketplace that allows select suppliers to sell directly to Costco's nearly 100 million members.

LiveChannel's "3rd Way" philosophy combines the best elements of DTC commerce (brand ownership, direct customer relationships, margin control) with marketplace commerce (built-in audience, trust, discovery). Rather than forcing brands into one model or the other, LiveChannel provides the platform, connectors, and services to operate in both simultaneously.

## How It Works

LiveChannel's connected commerce model operates differently from traditional affiliate networks:

1. **Community-Brand Connection**: LiveChannel connects large membership communities (like Costco's 100M+ members) with select brands, creating exclusive shopping experiences.
2. **Supplier Storefronts**: Each Costco Next supplier operates their own Shopify storefront, branded for the Costco Next experience. The supplier is the merchant of record, handles fulfillment, and owns the customer relationship.
3. **Commission-Based Revenue Sharing**: Suppliers pay Costco a commission on all net sales made on their Costco Next Shopify storefront, functioning similarly to an affiliate commission but within a closed community.
4. **Cross-Channel Integration**: The LiveChannel Platform connects multiple channels within Costco, including Costco Next online, warehouse displays, and product packaging, creating a unified commerce experience.
5. **Integration Instructions**: The platform allows users to create integration "instructions" that take transaction information and securely route it between partner systems, enabling seamless cross-platform commerce.

## Key Products & Services

### LiveChannel Platform
The core connected commerce platform that enables community-brand marketplace experiences. Handles storefronts, integrations, transaction tracking, monetization, and reporting.

### CX Connector App
The LiveChannel CX Connector App provides the technology bridge between suppliers and the Costco Next ecosystem, managing product catalog sync, order routing, and commission tracking.

### Partner Services Portal
Tools for suppliers to manage their presence in the connected commerce ecosystem, including performance analytics, integration management, and reporting.

### Channel Integration Services
Consulting and technical services to design, build, and maintain connected commerce solutions that span online, in-store, and packaging touchpoints.

## Pricing & Commission Model

LiveChannel operates on a partnership/commission model rather than a traditional SaaS subscription:

- **Commission Structure**: Suppliers pay the community partner (e.g., Costco) a commission on net sales generated through their connected storefront.
- **Platform Fees**: LiveChannel charges platform fees for its technology and services, though specific pricing is negotiated individually.
- **Integration Services**: Custom pricing for design, build, and maintenance of connected commerce solutions.

The model aligns incentives: LiveChannel and the community partner only earn when suppliers make sales, creating a performance-based relationship.

## Strengths

- **Costco Ecosystem Access**: The ability to sell directly to Costco's nearly 100 million members is an extraordinary advantage that no other platform offers.
- **Unique Model**: The "3rd Way" connected commerce approach is genuinely different from traditional affiliate networks or marketplace aggregators, offering brands more control than a marketplace while providing more traffic than pure DTC.
- **Merchant-of-Record Ownership**: Suppliers retain ownership of the customer relationship, pricing, and fulfillment, unlike traditional marketplaces where the platform controls the experience.
- **Trust Transfer**: Brands benefit from the trust and loyalty of the community (e.g., Costco members), which dramatically reduces customer acquisition costs.
- **Multi-Channel Potential**: The platform's ability to connect online, in-store, and packaging channels creates a truly omnichannel commerce experience.

## Weaknesses

- **Single Client Concentration**: LiveChannel's primary public deployment is Costco Next, creating significant client concentration risk.
- **Limited Scale**: With only $1.99M in funding raised, LiveChannel is significantly smaller than competitors. The platform's reach depends on its community partners.
- **Opaque Operations**: Limited public information about the platform's technology, merchant count, or performance metrics makes evaluation difficult.
- **Invitation-Only Access**: Suppliers must be selected by Costco to participate in Costco Next, creating a high barrier to entry.
- **Nascent Market**: The "connected commerce" category is still being defined, and it is unclear how quickly LiveChannel can expand beyond Costco.

## Recent Developments

- **Costco Next Expansion**: Costco Next continues to expand its supplier roster, adding new brands across electronics, home goods, outdoor, and specialty categories.
- **Multi-Channel Integration**: LiveChannel has been connecting additional Costco touchpoints, including warehouse displays and product packaging, to the digital storefront experience.
- **Shopify Integration**: The platform's reliance on Shopify storefronts for Costco Next suppliers aligns with the broader e-commerce ecosystem and makes onboarding relatively straightforward for brands already on Shopify.

## Market Position & Competitive Context

LiveChannel does not compete directly with traditional affiliate networks. Instead, it occupies a unique niche as "connected commerce" infrastructure:

- **LiveChannel vs. Traditional Networks (CJ, Awin)**: Networks connect brands with publishers; LiveChannel connects brands with community members. The traffic source is fundamentally different: curated community members vs. open web publishers.
- **LiveChannel vs. Marketplaces (Amazon, Shopify)**: Unlike Amazon where the marketplace controls the customer relationship, LiveChannel lets brands retain DTC ownership while tapping into a community audience.
- **LiveChannel vs. Affiliate Platforms (Impact.com)**: Impact.com manages publisher partnerships at scale; LiveChannel manages community-brand connections through a single anchor client (Costco).

The Costco Next model could be replicated with other membership-based communities (warehouse clubs, credit unions, professional associations), but LiveChannel's limited funding and single-client concentration make expansion uncertain.

## Program Structure for Suppliers

For brands participating in Costco Next through LiveChannel, the operational model includes:

- **Shopify-Based Storefronts**: Each supplier operates a Costco-branded Shopify storefront, meaning existing Shopify expertise transfers directly.
- **Merchant of Record**: The supplier handles all fulfillment, returns, and customer service. This is DTC with a Costco wrapper.
- **Commission on Net Sales**: Suppliers pay Costco a commission (not disclosed publicly) on net sales through their storefront.
- **Member-Only Access**: Only verified Costco members can access Costco Next storefronts, creating an exclusive shopping environment.
- **Multi-Channel Touchpoints**: LiveChannel connects warehouse displays, product packaging, and digital storefronts into a unified experience.

## Vendor Takeaway

LiveChannel represents a fundamentally different approach to affiliate and partnership commerce. Rather than operating within a traditional network, brands that qualify for Costco Next gain direct access to one of the most valuable and loyal consumer communities in the world. For Flywheel vendors, LiveChannel is highly relevant as a model for how connected commerce could evolve: communities acting as curated gatekeepers, brands retaining DTC control while leveraging massive built-in audiences, and commissions flowing based on actual sales performance. The primary challenge is access, as Costco Next is invitation-only and selective. Vendors should monitor LiveChannel's expansion beyond Costco and consider whether the connected commerce model could be replicated with other community-based organizations.
