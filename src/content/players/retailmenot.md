## Overview

RetailMeNot is one of the largest digital coupon and deal aggregation platforms in the world. Founded in 2006 by Australian entrepreneurs Guy King and Bevan Clark, the platform has grown into a major force in the coupon and deal affiliate space, with 688 million annual visits, 500,000+ active offers from 70,000+ retailers, and over 23 million monthly visitors in the United States alone.

The company has had a notable ownership history: after going public, RetailMeNot was acquired by Harland Clarke (later Vericast) in 2017. In 2020, J2 Global acquired RetailMeNot from Vericast for approximately $420 million. Following J2 Global's corporate restructuring, RetailMeNot became part of Ziff Davis, the digital media company. RetailMeNot is headquartered in Austin, Texas, and operates as a performance-based publisher that earns commissions when consumers use deals and coupons sourced through its platform.

## How It Works

RetailMeNot operates as a coupon and deal aggregation publisher:

1. **Deal Aggregation**: RetailMeNot sources promotional codes, coupons, sales, cashback offers, and gift card deals from 70,000+ retailers. Deals are sourced through direct merchant relationships, affiliate networks, and community submissions.
2. **Consumer Discovery**: Users browse RetailMeNot's website, mobile app, or browser extension to find deals and coupons before making purchases.
3. **Click-Through Attribution**: When consumers use a RetailMeNot deal, they click through an affiliate-tracked link to the retailer's site. The sale is attributed to RetailMeNot.
4. **Commission Earning**: RetailMeNot earns an affiliate commission from the retailer (or the affiliate network) for each attributed sale.
5. **Cashback Sharing**: In some cases, RetailMeNot shares a portion of its commission with the consumer as cashback, incentivizing use of the platform.

## Key Products & Services

### Coupon & Promo Code Directory
The core product: a searchable directory of 500,000+ deals, coupons, and promotional codes across 70,000+ retailers.

### RetailMeNot Deal Finder (Browser Extension)
A browser extension that automatically surfaces relevant deals and coupon codes as users shop online, applying available discounts at checkout.

### Mobile App
iOS and Android apps for on-the-go deal discovery, including in-store offers with location-based activation.

### Cashback Program
Select offers include cashback rewards, where RetailMeNot shares a portion of its affiliate commission with the consumer.

### Merchant Solutions
Direct programs for retailers to promote their offers prominently on RetailMeNot, with featured placement and targeted promotion options.

## Pricing & Commission Model

RetailMeNot earns revenue through performance-based affiliate commissions:

- **Affiliate Commissions**: RetailMeNot earns standard affiliate commissions (typically CPA) from retailers through affiliate networks when consumers use deals and make purchases.
- **Featured Placement**: Retailers can pay for premium placement of their offers on RetailMeNot, increasing visibility to the platform's 23M+ monthly US visitors.
- **Merchant Solutions**: Direct advertising and promotional programs for retailers wanting enhanced exposure on the platform.
- **Cashback Costs**: When offering cashback to consumers, RetailMeNot absorbs a portion of its commission as a customer acquisition/retention cost.

## Strengths

- **Massive Traffic Volume**: 688M+ annual visits and 23M+ monthly US visitors provide enormous reach for merchant offers.
- **Intent-Driven Traffic**: Users visiting RetailMeNot are actively looking to make purchases, creating high-intent traffic with strong conversion potential.
- **Retailer Coverage**: 70,000+ retailers and 500,000+ offers mean consumers can find deals for virtually any online store.
- **Multi-Channel Presence**: Website, mobile app, and browser extension capture consumers at different points in the shopping journey.
- **Brand Recognition**: RetailMeNot is one of the best-known coupon brands among US consumers.
- **Ziff Davis Backing**: Ownership by a major digital media company provides resources and cross-promotion opportunities.

## Weaknesses

- **Attribution Controversy**: Coupon and deal sites are frequently criticized for claiming attribution on sales that would have happened anyway (the customer was already going to buy and just searched for a coupon).
- **Last-Click Bias**: RetailMeNot benefits from last-click attribution models, which credit the final touchpoint (the coupon click) rather than the content or advertising that drove initial purchase intent.
- **Margin Erosion**: By promoting discounts, RetailMeNot can erode merchant margins and train consumers to seek coupons before every purchase.
- **Browser Extension Competition**: The Deal Finder browser extension competes with Honey (PayPal), Capital One Shopping, and other coupon extensions that have faced industry scrutiny for attribution manipulation.
- **Quality Control**: Community-submitted coupon codes may be expired, fake, or misleading, damaging consumer trust.
- **Merchant Control**: Retailers have limited control over which offers appear on RetailMeNot, and competitors' deals may appear alongside their own.

## Recent Developments

- **Ziff Davis Integration**: Continued integration into the Ziff Davis digital media portfolio, with cross-promotion opportunities across other Ziff Davis properties.
- **Cashback Expansion**: Expanded cashback offerings to compete more directly with Rakuten Rewards and other cashback platforms.
- **Mobile Growth**: Continued investment in the mobile app experience, including location-based in-store offer activation.
- **Industry Scrutiny**: The broader affiliate industry continues to debate the value of coupon/deal publishers, with some networks implementing stricter attribution controls.

## Vendor Takeaway

RetailMeNot represents a double-edged sword for vendors. On one hand, it drives massive volume traffic from high-intent consumers. On the other, it can erode margins and claim attribution on sales that would have happened regardless. For Flywheel vendors, the key considerations are: (1) evaluate whether RetailMeNot traffic genuinely incremental or merely capturing existing purchase intent at the last click; (2) consider implementing new-customer-only commission structures to ensure RetailMeNot is driving acquisition, not just discounting existing customers; (3) monitor the broader industry trend toward stricter attribution standards and reduced coupon site commissions. RetailMeNot is most valuable for vendors during promotional periods (Black Friday, clearance events) when the goal is maximum volume rather than margin protection.
