## Overview

The Interactive Advertising Bureau (IAB) is the preeminent global trade organization for the digital advertising industry. Founded in 1996 and headquartered in New York City, the IAB sets standards and best practices for digital advertising, including ad formats, measurement methodologies, privacy frameworks, and transparency guidelines. The organization's membership comprises more than 700 leading media companies, brands, agencies, and technology firms responsible for selling, delivering, and optimizing digital advertising campaigns.

Led by CEO David Cohen, with the 2025 Board of Directors chaired by Shenan Reed (Global Chief Media Officer, General Motors), the IAB operates through expert-led committees and working groups that develop the technical and policy frameworks underpinning the digital advertising ecosystem. While affiliate marketing is not the IAB's primary focus, the organization's standards, privacy frameworks, and AI transparency initiatives increasingly impact how performance marketing operates.

The IAB also operates IAB Tech Lab, a separate entity focused on developing technical standards and specifications for digital advertising.

## How It Works

The IAB operates as a membership-driven standards body:

1. **Standards Development**: Expert-led committees develop technical specifications, guidelines, and best practices for digital advertising. Members participate in working groups and contribute to standard creation.
2. **Research & Reporting**: The IAB publishes comprehensive research on digital advertising spending, trends, and market dynamics, providing authoritative industry data.
3. **Events & Education**: Major annual events including the IAB Annual Leadership Meeting, NewFronts, and PlayFronts, plus educational programs and certifications.
4. **Advocacy**: The IAB represents the digital advertising industry in policy and regulatory discussions, particularly around data privacy, content moderation, and AI governance.
5. **Compliance Frameworks**: The IAB develops compliance frameworks (like the Transparency and Consent Framework) that members and the broader industry implement to meet regulatory requirements.

## Key Products & Services

### IAB New Standard Ad Unit Portfolio
Detailed guidelines for digital advertising formats across websites, mobile apps, social media, and emerging media types including virtual and augmented reality.

### Transparency and Consent Framework (TCF)
A privacy compliance framework for managing user consent and transparency in digital advertising. TCF v2.3, released in June 2025, focuses on improved vendor disclosure, with compliance expected by February 2026.

### AI Transparency and Disclosure Framework (2026)
The IAB's first-ever framework for AI disclosure in advertising, addressing transparency requirements as AI and agentic systems become central to digital advertising and commerce.

### IAB Tech Lab Standards
Technical specifications for ad serving, measurement, viewability, and fraud prevention, developed through the IAB Tech Lab.

### Market Research
Comprehensive digital advertising spending reports and market analyses, including the finding that the European digital advertising market grew 16% to $118.9 billion in 2025.

### Events
Major industry events including the Annual Leadership Meeting, NewFronts (digital video upfronts), and PlayFronts (gaming advertising).

## Pricing & Commission Model

The IAB is a membership-based trade organization:

- **General Membership**: Annual dues based on company revenue, providing access to committees, research, events, and standards development processes.
- **IAB Tech Lab Membership**: Separate membership for participation in technical standards development.
- **Event Sponsorship**: Sponsorship and attendance fees for IAB events.
- **Research Access**: Some research publications are available to members only, while others are published publicly.

## Strengths

- **Standards Authority**: IAB standards are the de facto benchmarks for the digital advertising industry. Compliance with IAB standards is often a prerequisite for operating in the ecosystem.
- **Privacy Framework Leadership**: The Transparency and Consent Framework (TCF) is the dominant compliance framework for GDPR and digital advertising privacy regulations.
- **AI Transparency Pioneer**: The 2026 AI Transparency and Disclosure Framework positions the IAB at the forefront of governing AI in advertising.
- **700+ Members**: Broad membership including all major players in digital advertising ensures that IAB standards reflect industry consensus.
- **Research Authority**: IAB market research is the authoritative source for digital advertising spending and trends globally.
- **Cross-Industry Influence**: IAB standards impact not just traditional advertising but increasingly affect affiliate marketing, agentic commerce, and AI-driven product discovery.

## Weaknesses

- **Affiliate Marketing Gap**: Affiliate and performance marketing have historically been underrepresented in IAB standards and research. The PMA is the more directly relevant trade body for affiliate-specific issues.
- **Slow Standard Adoption**: Industry-consensus standards development is inherently slow, which can lag behind rapid innovation in AI commerce and agentic protocols.
- **Large Company Bias**: The 700+ member base is dominated by large media companies, brands, and agencies. Smaller affiliate publishers and niche platforms have less voice.
- **Complexity**: IAB standards and frameworks are technically complex, creating implementation challenges for smaller organizations.
- **Regional Variation**: While the IAB operates globally (IAB Europe, IAB UK, etc.), standards can vary by region, creating compliance complexity for international operations.

## Recent Developments

- **AI Transparency and Disclosure Framework (2026)**: Released the first-ever industry framework for AI disclosure in advertising, addressing the rapid growth of AI in ad creation, targeting, and commerce.
- **Agentic AI Focus (2026)**: The IAB has centered its 2026 agenda on Agentic AI, recognizing that autonomous AI systems that plan, decide, and act are fundamentally changing digital advertising and commerce.
- **TCF v2.3 (2025)**: Released updated Transparency and Consent Framework focusing on improved vendor disclosure, with compliance deadline of February 2026.
- **Shenan Reed Board Chair (2025)**: Appointed General Motors' Global Chief Media Officer as Board Chair, signaling brand-side leadership in IAB governance.
- **European Market Growth**: Reported that the European digital advertising market reached $118.9 billion in 2025, a 16% increase.
