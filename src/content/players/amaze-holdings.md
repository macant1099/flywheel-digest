## Overview

Amaze Holdings, Inc. (NYSE American: AMZE) is a publicly traded, end-to-end creator-powered commerce platform headquartered in Newport Beach, California. Founded in 2018 and led by CEO Aaron Day, Amaze enables creators, influencers, and community sellers to "sell anything, anywhere" — providing tools for product creation, storefronts, e-commerce operations, and now live shopping at scale.

The company operates at remarkable scale: a global network of more than **137 million creators** across **160+ countries**, driving over **200 million storefront visits** per month and reaching an estimated **1.7 billion fans worldwide**. Unlike engagement-metric-focused platforms, Amaze captures verified purchase behavior across millions of creators and products, creating a first-party commerce dataset built on what consumers actually buy — not what they click or like.

In 2026, Amaze has accelerated its evolution from a pure creator storefront tool into a full creator commerce media company, launching three major initiatives: **Amaze Live** (live shopping), the **Creator Commerce Media Platform** (first-party data monetization via DSP), and a **Creator Intelligence** scoring system for brands. The company is NYSE-listed and publicly reporting, making it an unusual transparent window into the creator commerce category.

## How It Works

Amaze operates across three interconnected layers:

1. **Creator Storefronts**: Creators build shoppable product pages (for merchandise, brand drops, digital products) using Amaze's tools. These storefronts are the core data capture layer — every visit, browse, and purchase generates first-party audience intelligence.

2. **Amaze Live**: A multi-channel live shopping platform connecting brand inventory with creators for real-time shoppable broadcasts. Creators host live selling events across supported social and owned channels, earning commission on sales (10% base). Brands supply inventory, gain access to live creative formats, and benefit from attribution tied to actual conversions.

3. **Creator Commerce Media Platform**: A data monetization layer built on Amaze's first-party purchase data. Brands and agencies access audience segments, creator performance scoring, and media activation through Amaze's proprietary DSP (integrated with The Trade Desk). Revenue streams include DSP audience activation, SaaS subscription data products, data-driven brand activation, and enterprise creator intelligence tools.

## Key Products & Services

### Creator Storefronts
The foundational product — a no-code platform enabling any creator to build a branded shoppable storefront selling physical merchandise, brand partnerships, and digital products. Over 137 million creators have used Amaze tools globally.

### Amaze Live
A live shopping and community commerce platform launched in 2026. Connects brands with creator talent for live, shoppable broadcasts across multiple channels. Key features:
- 10% base commission for approved creators on qualifying product sales
- Access to professional live-streaming studios in Los Angeles, Carlsbad, Miami, and expanding markets
- Opportunities for brand collaborations and sponsored campaigns
- Multi-channel streaming across supported platforms
- First campaigns expected on or before May 15, 2026; 20+ brands committed at launch

### Creator Commerce Media Platform (CCMP)
A new high-margin revenue layer announced March 2026, converting Amaze's first-party purchase data into:
- **DSP Activation**: Audience segments built on verified purchase behavior, activated through Amaze's DSP and syndicated to The Trade Desk
- **Subscription Data Products (SaaS)**: Aggregated conversion intelligence for brands, agencies, and creator management teams
- **Data-Driven Brand Activation**: Closed-loop campaigns within Amaze's ecosystem with full purchase attribution
- **Creator Intelligence**: Enterprise scoring model ranking creators by transaction performance (not follower count), enabling data-driven creator selection

### Managed Services
Full-service managed commerce programs for creators and brands requiring operational support — product sourcing, fulfillment, customer service, and campaign management.

## Pricing & Commission Model

Amaze's revenue model spans several streams:

- **Creator Commission (Amaze Live)**: 10% base commission on qualifying product sales. Brands fund the commission as a cost of creator partnership.
- **Platform Fees**: SaaS subscriptions for access to storefront tools, analytics, and CCMP data products (pricing not publicly disclosed).
- **DSP Revenue**: Media spend-based revenue from brand campaigns activated through the Amaze DSP.
- **Managed Services**: Project-based or retainer fees for managed commerce programs.
- **Enterprise Solutions**: Custom pricing for enterprise-level creator intelligence and campaign programs.

The company is publicly traded and reports financials quarterly — unusual for creator commerce platforms and a source of transparency on category economics.

## Strengths

- **Scale Nobody Else Has**: 137M+ creator network across 160+ countries with 1.7B fan reach is unprecedented in creator commerce. The raw distribution potential for brand partners is enormous.
- **First-Party Purchase Data**: Unlike social platforms that sell engagement signals, Amaze captures verified purchase behavior. In a cookie-less world, this is increasingly valuable.
- **Full-Funnel Architecture**: Amaze now covers discovery (social/live), storefront (conversion), and data monetization (CCMP) — a complete creator commerce stack.
- **Public Company Transparency**: NYSE-listed with quarterly reporting gives brands and partners visibility into financial health and trajectory.
- **Live Shopping at Scale**: Amaze Live's professional studio infrastructure (LA, Carlsbad, Miami) lowers the barrier for brands to produce high-quality live selling content.
- **Trade Desk Integration**: CCMP audiences accessible through The Trade Desk puts Amaze data into the hands of enterprise media buyers.
- **Creator Intelligence Scoring**: Transaction-based creator scoring is a genuine advancement over follower-count selection methods.

## Weaknesses

- **Early-Stage Revenue Scaling**: Despite massive creator scale, Amaze is still working to translate its network into consistent, predictable revenue. Live campaigns start May 2026 — the economics are unproven at scale.
- **Execution Risk on Multiple Fronts**: Simultaneously launching Amaze Live, CCMP, and DSP products is ambitious. Multi-product scaling risks diluting focus.
- **Brand Awareness**: Despite NYSE listing and creator scale, Amaze is not a top-of-mind brand in the affiliate/performance marketing conversation. Discovery happens through creator channels, not marketer outreach.
- **Commission Pressure**: 10% creator commissions are competitive but could face downward pressure if the category matures or larger platforms (TikTok Shop, Instagram Shopping) recruit the same creators with better terms.
- **Platform Dependency**: Multi-channel live streaming relies on third-party social platforms (who can change terms, restrict access, or build competing products).
- **Micro-Cap Risk**: As a small NYSE-listed company, Amaze is subject to capital markets volatility that larger private competitors avoid.

## Competitive Position

Amaze competes in multiple overlapping categories:

- **vs. LTK (LikeToKnow.it)**: Both serve creator storefronts and brand partnerships. LTK is more established in the fashion/lifestyle creator segment; Amaze's global scale (160+ countries) and live shopping capabilities are differentiators.
- **vs. ShopMy**: ShopMy focuses on curated creator storefronts for the luxury/prestige segment. Amaze's volume (137M creators) is dramatically larger but skews more broadly.
- **vs. TikTok Shop Creator Program**: TikTok offers massive built-in discovery but is platform-locked. Amaze's multi-channel approach avoids single-platform dependency.
- **vs. Amazon Live**: Amazon Live is Amazon-native and affiliate-linked. Amaze Live is multi-brand, multi-channel, and creator-relationship driven.
- **vs. Whatnot**: Whatnot specializes in live auctions (collectibles, entertainment). Amaze Live targets broader brand commerce.
