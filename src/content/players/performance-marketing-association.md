## Overview

The Performance Marketing Association (PMA) is the affiliate marketing industry's primary trade association — the body that produces authoritative market sizing research, sets ethical standards, and represents the industry's interests in regulatory conversations. Founded in 2008, it serves as the connective tissue between the fragmented ecosystem of networks, platforms, publishers, and advertisers.

For LiveChannel vendors, the PMA's primary value is **intelligence**: their annual Industry Study produces the most credible, methodology-transparent market sizing data available for affiliate marketing, drawing from direct data submissions by participating networks and platforms. When anyone cites "the affiliate industry is worth $X billion," they're usually citing PMA research.

Understanding the PMA's findings is essential for benchmarking program performance, justifying affiliate channel investment internally, and understanding market dynamics.

## Key Activities

**Industry Research & Market Sizing**
The PMA's most important output is its periodic Industry Study. The 2025 report (released June 2025) found:
- U.S. affiliate marketing spend grew **49.8%** from $9.1B (2021) to **$13.63B** in 2024
- Affiliate marketing generated **$113 billion** in e-commerce sales during 2024
- Industry CAGR of **14.42%** — twice the pace of broader e-commerce growth
- Affiliate spend is projected to continue growing as AI search reshapes discovery and more brands move to performance-based marketing

**Standards & Guidelines**
The PMA publishes guidelines on disclosure, FTC compliance, fraud prevention, and tracking standards. These effectively become industry norms that networks and platforms align to.

**Regulatory Advocacy**
The PMA represents the industry in discussions around consumer privacy legislation, FTC disclosure rules, and cookie/tracking regulation — outcomes that directly affect affiliate tracking infrastructure.

**Education & Certification**
Produces educational content, webinars, and certification programs for affiliate marketers and program managers.

**Annual Conference & Events**
Hosts events and participates in major industry conferences (including Affiliate Summit).

## Key Reports & Resources

**PMA Industry Study** — Definitive U.S. affiliate market size, growth rates, and vertical breakdowns. Published periodically (last: June 2025, covering 2024 data). The gold standard for industry benchmarking.

**Transparency & Trust Reports** — Data on fraud prevalence, disclosure compliance rates, and industry health metrics.

**Publisher Perspective Reports** — Survey-based research on publisher priorities, technology adoption, and partnership preferences.

## Membership

PMA membership is open to networks, platforms, agencies, brands, and publishers operating in the performance marketing space. Membership tiers vary by company size and revenue, with benefits including event access, industry report early access, and advocacy representation.

Notable member-contributors to the Industry Study: CJ Affiliate, Awin, ShareASale, impact.com, Rakuten Advertising, and other major networks participating in aggregate data contribution.

## Recent Developments

**June 2025** — Released the PMA Industry Study 2025, the most comprehensive affiliate market sizing ever conducted. Key headline: 49.8% growth to $13.63B, outpacing e-commerce by 2x. This report significantly raised affiliate marketing's profile in boardroom investment discussions across retail and DTC.

**2025** — Increased focus on AI's impact on affiliate tracking, publisher discovery, and attribution as the industry navigates the transition from cookie-based tracking and SEO-driven publisher traffic.

**2026** — PMA expected to release guidance on agentic commerce and AI shopping agent affiliate attribution — a frontier question for the entire industry.

## Vendor Takeaway

The PMA is not a direct operational partner for vendors — you don't run your affiliate program through the PMA. Its value is **strategic intelligence and credibility**:

1. **Use PMA data** to benchmark your affiliate program's performance against industry averages and justify channel investment to leadership
2. **Monitor PMA guidance** on FTC disclosure, tracking standards, and AI attribution — these will shape compliance requirements across the industry
3. **Consider membership** if you're building a serious affiliate channel and want a seat at the table for regulatory discussions affecting the industry

The $13.63B / $113B e-commerce attribution stats from the 2025 study are the most credible numbers available for making the case for affiliate investment internally.
