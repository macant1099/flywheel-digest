## Overview

Rakuten Rewards (formerly Ebates) is the largest free cashback loyalty platform in the United States. Founded in 1998 in Menlo Park, California, as Ebates by former deputy district attorneys Alessandro Isolani and Paul Wasserman, the company was acquired by Rakuten Group (the Japanese internet conglomerate) in 2014 for $1 billion. The rebrand from Ebates to Rakuten Rewards was completed in 2019.

Headquartered in San Mateo, California, Rakuten Rewards has over 17 million US members who have collectively earned more than $3.7 billion in cashback. The platform partners with over 3,500 retailers, including major brands like Walmart, Amazon, Macy's, Sephora, Best Buy, and Lululemon. As both a consumer loyalty platform and a major affiliate publisher, Rakuten Rewards sits at a unique intersection: it drives massive retail volume while providing a built-in loyalty channel that merchants can activate through the Rakuten Advertising network.

## How It Works

Rakuten Rewards operates as a cashback loyalty publisher:

1. **Member Registration**: Consumers sign up for a free Rakuten Rewards account. New members receive a signup bonus (currently up to $50 or 5,000 loyalty points).
2. **Shopping Portal**: Members visit Rakuten.com or use the browser extension before shopping. They select a retailer and click through to the merchant's site via an affiliate-tracked link.
3. **Cashback Earning**: When the member completes a purchase, Rakuten earns an affiliate commission from the retailer. Rakuten shares a portion of this commission with the member as cashback (typically 1-10%, sometimes higher during promotions).
4. **Payment**: Cashback accumulates and is paid quarterly via check, PayPal, or converted to American Express Membership Rewards points or Bilt Rewards Points.
5. **In-Store Cashback**: Members can link a payment card to their Rakuten account and earn cashback on qualifying in-store purchases by activating offers before shopping.

## Key Products & Services

### Online Cashback Portal
The core product: a shopping portal where members earn cashback at 3,500+ online retailers by clicking through Rakuten before purchasing.

### Rakuten Cash Back Button (Browser Extension)
A browser extension that automatically notifies members of available cashback when they visit a partner retailer's website, applying tracking without requiring a portal visit.

### In-Store Cashback
Card-linked offers that enable members to earn cashback at physical retail locations by linking their credit or debit card to their Rakuten account.

### Rakuten Credit Card
A co-branded credit card offering an extra 4% cashback on top of portal cashback (on up to $7,000 in annual spending), plus 1% on all other purchases.

### Points Conversion
Members can choose to receive rewards as cashback, American Express Membership Rewards points, or Bilt Rewards Points, providing flexibility for different loyalty program preferences.

### Referral Program
Members earn bonuses (currently up to $50 per referral) for inviting friends, with the referred friend also receiving a bonus.

## Pricing & Commission Model

For merchants, Rakuten Rewards operates as an affiliate publisher:

- **Affiliate Commission**: Merchants pay standard affiliate commissions (CPA) to Rakuten Rewards through affiliate networks (primarily Rakuten Advertising, but also CJ, Impact.com, etc.).
- **Commission Sharing**: Rakuten keeps a portion of the affiliate commission and passes the remainder to the member as cashback.
- **Featured Placement**: Merchants can pay for premium placement and increased cashback rates during promotional periods to drive volume.
- **No Direct Fees to Members**: The platform is free for consumers. Revenue comes entirely from affiliate commissions.

## Strengths

- **Massive Loyal Audience**: 17M+ US members who actively choose to shop through Rakuten create a captive, high-intent audience.
- **Consumer Loyalty**: The cashback model creates strong habitual behavior. Members return to Rakuten before every purchase.
- **Retailer Coverage**: 3,500+ retailer partners cover virtually every major online shopping category.
- **Multi-Channel**: Online portal, browser extension, in-store cashback, and credit card create multiple touchpoints in the shopping journey.
- **Rakuten Ecosystem**: Integration with Rakuten Group's global ecosystem (1.4B members worldwide) provides unique cross-promotion opportunities.
- **Points Flexibility**: The ability to convert cashback to Amex or Bilt points appeals to the lucrative points-and-miles community.
- **Golden State Warriors Sponsorship**: Jersey badge sponsorship provides mainstream brand awareness.

## Weaknesses

- **Attribution Concerns**: Like all cashback platforms, Rakuten Rewards faces questions about incrementality. Members may have purchased regardless; the cashback merely captures last-click attribution.
- **Margin Cost to Merchants**: Paying affiliate commissions that are then shared as cashback effectively means merchants are paying for loyalty they may already have.
- **Quarterly Payment Cycle**: Cashback is paid quarterly (not immediately), which can frustrate members accustomed to instant rewards.
- **Browser Extension Competition**: The Cash Back Button competes with Honey, Capital One Shopping, and other browser extensions for the "last-click" position.
- **Rakuten Network Pressure**: Merchants on the Rakuten Advertising network may feel pressure to offer Rakuten Rewards activation as part of their affiliate program.

## Market Position & Competitive Context

Rakuten Rewards is the dominant cashback platform in the US market:

- **Rakuten Rewards vs. RetailMeNot**: RetailMeNot focuses on coupons; Rakuten focuses on cashback. Rakuten's model creates stronger loyalty (members return habitually) while RetailMeNot is more transactional.
- **Rakuten Rewards vs. Capital One Shopping**: Capital One Shopping is free and earns points toward gift cards. Rakuten offers more flexible rewards (cash, Amex points, Bilt points). Both compete for the browser extension position.
- **Rakuten Rewards vs. Honey (PayPal)**: Honey automatically applies coupon codes; Rakuten provides cashback on purchases. Different value propositions that increasingly overlap.
- **Rakuten Rewards vs. TopCashback/BeFrugal**: Smaller cashback competitors that often offer higher rates on individual merchants but lack Rakuten's scale and brand recognition.

Rakuten's integration with the Rakuten Advertising network creates a unique advantage: merchants on Rakuten Advertising can seamlessly activate Rakuten Rewards as a publisher, creating a frictionless path from network membership to cashback channel activation.

## Cashback Economics for Vendors

Understanding the economics of cashback publishing is essential:

- **Commission Split**: When a Rakuten member earns 5% cashback, the merchant may be paying 8-12% in total affiliate commission. Rakuten keeps the difference (the "take rate").
- **Promotional Cashback**: Merchants can increase cashback rates during key periods (e.g., 10% cashback during Black Friday) to drive volume. This comes from increased merchant commissions, not from Rakuten absorbing margin.
- **Incrementality Debate**: The same incrementality questions that apply to RetailMeNot apply to Rakuten Rewards: habitual Rakuten users may have purchased regardless. However, the loyalty effect of regular cashback creates genuine repeat purchase behavior that may not exist otherwise.
- **New Customer Acquisition**: Rakuten's referral program (currently $50 per referral) actively grows the member base, providing merchants with access to genuinely new customers entering the ecosystem.

## Vendor Takeaway

Rakuten Rewards is one of the most important affiliate publishers for any consumer brand with an online presence. Its 17M+ members represent a significant and engaged shopping audience that actively seeks out deals and cashback before purchasing. For Flywheel vendors, the key considerations are: (1) activate Rakuten Rewards as a publisher in your affiliate program, ideally through the Rakuten Advertising network for seamless integration; (2) offer competitive cashback rates to ensure visibility among the 3,500+ competing retailers; (3) use promotional cashback increases strategically during key shopping periods to drive volume; (4) evaluate incrementality carefully, as some Rakuten traffic may not be truly incremental to your existing sales. Despite attribution concerns, Rakuten Rewards is too large and influential to ignore, and the loyalty effect of regular cashback creates genuine repeat purchase behavior.
