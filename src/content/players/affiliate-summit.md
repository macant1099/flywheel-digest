## Overview

Affiliate Summit is the industry's most important event and media brand, hosting two flagship annual conferences — Affiliate Summit West (Las Vegas, January) and Affiliate Summit East (New York, July) — that collectively attract the largest concentration of affiliate industry participants on the planet. Founded in 2003 by Missy Ward and Shawn Collins, Affiliate Summit has been the pulse of the affiliate industry for over two decades.

For LiveChannel vendors, Affiliate Summit serves two primary functions:
1. **Market intelligence**: The publications, reports (especially the Top 25 Publishers Report), and conference content provide ground-level intel on what publishers want, what's working, and where the industry is moving
2. **Relationship building**: ASW and ASE are where program managers meet publishers, where deals are struck, and where networks demo new products — the single highest-ROI event for affiliate relationship development

## Key Events

**Affiliate Summit West (ASW)** — Las Vegas, January
The industry's flagship gathering. Attracts publishers, advertisers, networks, platforms, agencies, and technology vendors. Multiple tracks of educational content plus an enormous expo floor. 2025 saw six-figure partnership deals closed on-site. ASW is where advertisers get face time with major publishers who are otherwise inaccessible.

**Affiliate Summit East (ASE)** — New York, July
Free for publishers and media buyers (Affiliate Summit explicitly does not charge affiliates to attend — they are "the heart and soul of the industry"). More intimate than ASW; strong for East Coast brands and relationship continuation from ASW.

## Key Publications & Reports

**Top 25 Publishers Report** — The most valuable piece of intelligence Affiliate Summit produces. An annual exclusive ranking of the top 25 affiliate publishers in the U.S. by volume and influence. Acquisition of this report effectively gives program managers a target list of the publishers they should be pursuing for their programs. Released in conjunction with ASE.

**Affiliate Summit Blog** — Regular industry content covering trends, case studies, and best practices.

**Podcast / Media** — Educational content distributed through multiple media channels targeting affiliate marketing professionals.

## Who Attends

- **Publishers/Affiliates**: Content sites, coupon and cashback apps, email marketers, social media influencers, loyalty platforms, review sites
- **Advertisers/Brands**: E-commerce companies, DTC brands, subscription services, financial products, travel companies
- **Networks & Platforms**: CJ, Awin, impact.com, PartnerStack, and virtually every major network maintains a presence
- **Agencies**: OPM (Outsourced Program Management) firms, digital marketing agencies with affiliate practices
- **Technology vendors**: Tracking software, fraud detection, attribution platforms

## Strengths

- **Publisher concentration**: The highest density of top-tier affiliate publishers in one place; no other event comes close for advertiser-publisher introductions
- **Top 25 Report**: Exclusive publisher ranking that would otherwise require months of research to approximate
- **Network neutrality**: Affiliate Summit serves the whole industry, not any specific network — makes it a trusted hub for objective industry temperature-taking
- **Free for publishers**: Removing the financial barrier for publishers ensures the attendee mix heavily includes the people advertisers most want to meet

## Weaknesses

- **Cost for advertisers**: Booth space at ASW can run $10,000–$100,000+; premium sponsorships are six figures
- **Scale can mean noise**: Large conferences require significant networking strategy; attending without a plan yields poor ROI
- **East Coast / U.S. focus**: ASW and ASE are heavily U.S.-centric; international affiliate relationships typically require attending regional events (European affiliate conferences, etc.)

## Recent Developments

**2025 ASW** — Featured significant content around AI's impact on affiliate publishing, GEO (Generative Engine Optimization), and creator affiliate as major themes. Six-figure partnership deals reported closed on the show floor.

**2025 ASE** — Released the Top 25 Publishers Report (April 2025), the annual definitive ranking of U.S. affiliate publishers by performance and influence.

**2026** — ASW 2026 (January) expected to feature AI commerce, agentic affiliate, and creator-affiliate convergence as dominant themes, reflecting the industry's evolution.

## Vendor Takeaway

Every affiliate program manager should attend **at least one Affiliate Summit per year** — ideally ASW for the publisher introductions, ASE for the Top 25 report release and East Coast relationships.

**Tactical advice:**
- Get the Top 25 Publishers Report every year — it's the most concentrated intelligence on who the top publishers are and what they need
- Pre-schedule publisher meetings before the event; the floor is chaotic and the best publishers are booked out
- Budget for a booth at ASW if publisher recruitment is a priority — passive attendance without a presence yields less ROI than active exhibiting

**For smaller brands:** ASE (which is free for publishers) may provide better ROI than the more expensive ASW, as publisher-to-advertiser ratios tend to be more favorable.
