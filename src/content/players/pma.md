## Overview

The Performance Marketing Association (PMA) is the affiliate marketing industry's global trade association, founded in 2008. Incorporated in Delaware as a 501(c)(6) non-profit corporation, the PMA's mission is to champion and protect accountable performance marketing. The organization represents a multi-billion dollar marketing channel comprising more than 200,000 businesses and individuals.

The PMA has over 150 corporate members, maintains active Slack community channels with hundreds of participants, and hosts regular industry events including Happy Hours with approximately 100 attendees each. It serves as the primary voice of the affiliate and performance marketing industry in the United States, conducting research, publishing industry studies, and advocating for the interests of advertisers, publishers, networks, and technology platforms that operate within the performance marketing ecosystem.

## How It Works

The PMA operates as a membership-based trade organization:

1. **Membership**: Companies and individuals across the affiliate and performance marketing ecosystem join as members, gaining access to research, networking, and advocacy resources.
2. **Industry Research**: The PMA commissions and publishes industry studies that provide benchmark data on market size, growth, and trends.
3. **Advocacy**: The PMA represents the performance marketing industry's interests in policy discussions, regulatory matters, and industry standards development.
4. **Networking Events**: Regular events including conferences, Happy Hours, and roundtables facilitate connections between industry professionals.
5. **Education**: Resources, best practices, and guidelines for performance marketing professionals.

## Key Products & Services

### PMA Industry Study
The flagship research publication, providing comprehensive data on affiliate marketing spending, growth rates, and industry trends. The 2025 study revealed that affiliate marketing spending increased 49.8% from $9.1 billion in 2021 to $13.62 billion in 2024, representing a CAGR of 14.42%.

### Industry Events
Regular networking events, happy hours, and industry gatherings, including a significant presence at Affiliate Summit conferences.

### Advocacy & Policy
Representation of the performance marketing industry's interests in regulatory and policy discussions affecting affiliate marketing, data privacy, and digital advertising.

### Member Directory & Networking
Access to a network of 150+ corporate members and 200,000+ affiliated businesses for networking, partnership, and collaboration.

### Best Practices & Education
Industry guidelines, best practices documentation, and educational resources for performance marketing professionals.

## Pricing & Commission Model

The PMA is a membership-based non-profit:

- **Corporate Membership**: Annual dues based on company size and tier. Membership provides access to all PMA resources, events, and research.
- **Industry Champion Membership**: Premium tier for companies wanting greater visibility and engagement with the PMA's activities.
- **Individual Participation**: Individuals can engage through events and community channels.
- **Research Access**: Major research publications (like the Industry Study) may be available to members or published publicly.

## Strengths

- **Industry Benchmark Data**: The PMA Industry Study is the definitive source for affiliate marketing market size and growth data, cited by virtually every industry analysis.
- **Collective Voice**: As the industry's trade association, the PMA provides a unified voice for advocacy on policy, regulation, and standards.
- **Networking Hub**: Regular events and active Slack channels facilitate connections between industry professionals across all segments.
- **Research Credibility**: Studies conducted by the PMA carry weight with brands, investors, and media as authoritative industry data.
- **Non-Profit Mission**: As a 501(c)(6), the PMA's mission is industry advancement rather than profit, lending credibility to its research and advocacy.

## Weaknesses

- **Limited Direct Impact**: As a trade body, the PMA influences policy and provides data but does not directly affect individual business outcomes.
- **US Focus**: While globally relevant, the PMA is primarily focused on the US market. European and Asian affiliate industries have their own associations.
- **Membership Concentration**: Corporate membership is weighted toward larger networks, platforms, and agencies, potentially underrepresenting smaller players and publishers.
- **Slow-Moving Advocacy**: Trade associations inherently move slower than the industry they represent, which can lag behind rapid changes in AI commerce and agentic protocols.
- **Awareness Gap**: Many brands engaged in affiliate marketing are unaware of the PMA's existence or resources.

## Vendor Takeaway

The PMA is a useful background resource for vendors entering the affiliate marketing space, but it is not a direct operational tool. The primary value for Flywheel vendors is: (1) the PMA Industry Study provides authoritative data on market size, growth, and trends that can inform business cases and strategic planning; (2) PMA events and Slack channels offer networking opportunities with affiliate networks, publishers, and technology platforms; (3) PMA advocacy helps shape the regulatory and policy environment for affiliate marketing. Vendors do not need to be PMA members to participate in affiliate marketing, but the organization's research and networking resources can be valuable for understanding the broader industry landscape.
