## Overview

Partnerize is an enterprise partnership automation platform that helps brands manage, track, and optimize their affiliate, influencer, and strategic partner programs at global scale. Founded in 2010 in Newcastle Upon Tyne, United Kingdom, by Peter Cheyne and Sean Sewell, the company has grown into a major player in the partnership management space, led by CEO Matt Gilbert.

Partnerize serves more than 1,800 enterprise and mid-market clients worldwide, driving over $10 billion in transactions through relationships with an ecosystem of 750,000+ partners, including 250,000+ influencers. The platform operates across 214 countries and territories, supporting 60 different currencies. The company has raised approximately $82.5 million in funding from investors including Accel-KKR, Mithril Capital, and GP Bullhound. As of February 28, 2026, Partnerize has 337 employees.

## How It Works

Partnerize positions itself as an end-to-end partnership automation platform:

1. **Partner Discovery**: AI-driven tools for identifying and recruiting relevant affiliates, influencers, and strategic partners from a pool of 750,000+.
2. **Contracting & Onboarding**: Automated workflows for partner agreements, terms, and onboarding processes.
3. **Tracking & Attribution**: Multi-touch attribution across devices and channels, with real-time reporting and analytics.
4. **Commission Management**: Dynamic commissioning based on customer value, product category, partner tier, and other variables.
5. **Payment**: Global payment processing in 60 currencies, with automated validation and disbursement.
6. **Brand Safety & Fraud Prevention**: Tools for monitoring partner compliance, detecting fraud, and protecting brand integrity.

## Key Products & Services

### Partnerize Platform
The core SaaS platform for managing all partnership types, with tracking, attribution, commissioning, and payment capabilities.

### VantagePoint for Publishers (January 2026)
An AI-driven attribution tool unveiled publicly at Affiliate Summit West and launched in limited release on January 14, 2026. It quantifies publisher authority across AI-mediated discovery and zero-click conversions — helping publishers prove and monetize influence even when no referral click exists. Extends the October 2025 VantagePoint launch (for brands) to address the publisher revenue gap created by AI search collapsing traditional click paths. Publishers get independent, auditable visibility into how their content influences downstream conversions, even when AI assistants handle the purchase decision.

### Pacesetters (February 2026)
A new charter program launched by Partnerize for elite publisher partners. The inaugural Pacesetters meeting took place on February 26, 2026. The program recognizes and works with publishers who demonstrate leadership in AI-mediated discovery and multi-touch influence across the partnership ecosystem.

### Konnecto AI (2025 Acquisition)
Following the acquisition of Konnecto, Partnerize integrated AI-powered competitive intelligence and customer journey mapping, enabling brands to discover competitor partnership strategies and identify new opportunities.

### Brand Safety & Fraud Prevention
Comprehensive compliance monitoring, fraud detection, and brand protection tools integrated into the platform.

### Managed Services
Full-service partnership management for brands that want strategic and operational support in running their programs.

## Pricing & Commission Model

Partnerize operates on an enterprise SaaS model:

- **Platform Fees**: Custom pricing based on program size, features, and support level. Typically enterprise-level pricing.
- **No Network Override**: As a SaaS platform, Partnerize does not take a percentage of commissions. Brands pay platform fees and commissions go directly to partners.
- **Managed Services**: Additional fees for hands-on program management and strategic services.
- **Revenue**: Reported approximately $65.7 million in revenue with 1,800+ customers as of 2024.

## Strengths

- **Global Scale**: 214 countries, 60 currencies, and 750,000+ partners make Partnerize a truly global solution.
- **Enterprise Focus**: Purpose-built for large, complex partnership programs with sophisticated commissioning, attribution, and compliance needs.
- **AI Innovation**: The Konnecto acquisition and VantagePoint for Publishers demonstrate investment in AI-driven partnership intelligence.
- **Publisher AI Search Attribution**: VantagePoint's ability to track content influence in AI search results (not just clicks) is forward-looking as AI search grows.
- **Comprehensive Fraud Prevention**: Integrated brand safety and fraud prevention reduce risk for enterprise advertisers.

## Weaknesses

- **Enterprise Pricing**: Custom, enterprise-level pricing makes Partnerize inaccessible for small and mid-market brands.
- **Complexity**: The platform's depth and enterprise features create a steeper learning curve compared to simpler solutions.
- **Market Positioning**: Competes directly with Impact.com for enterprise share but has lower brand awareness, particularly in the US market.
- **UK Heritage**: While globally capable, Partnerize's Newcastle headquarters and European roots can be a perception challenge for US-centric brands.
- **Funding Pressure**: With $82.5M raised, there may be investor pressure for exits or aggressive growth that could impact product focus.

## Vendor Takeaway

Partnerize is most relevant for enterprise brands running complex, global partnership programs that need sophisticated attribution, commissioning, and compliance capabilities. For Flywheel vendors, Partnerize is worth evaluating if they operate at enterprise scale across multiple countries and need advanced features like AI-driven competitive intelligence (via Konnecto) or publisher AI search attribution (via VantagePoint). The platform's VantagePoint tool is particularly noteworthy: as AI search grows, understanding how content influences purchases through AI interfaces (not just traditional clicks) will become critical. For mid-market vendors, Impact.com typically offers a better entry point with similar capabilities at more accessible pricing.
