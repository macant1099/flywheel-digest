## Overview

Wildfire Systems is an AI-powered affiliate monetization company that provides the infrastructure layer enabling any application, AI agent, or digital platform to earn affiliate revenue from commerce it drives. Founded in 2017 by Jordan Glazier (a former eBay executive who led the e-commerce giant's three largest marketplaces), the company is headquartered in Solana Beach, California.

Wildfire's flagship product, RevenueEngine, transforms product mentions from AI tools, chatbots, agents, and any content surface into monetized and trackable affiliate links across over 50,000 merchant programs. The company has raised $40.5 million in total funding, including a $15 million Series A (2021, co-led by TTV Capital and QED Investors) and a $16 million Series B (2024, co-led by Intuit Ventures and Mucker Capital). Notable investors include Samsung Next, Citi Ventures, B Capital, and Cohen Circle.

RevenueEngine won a 2025 Generative AI Expo Product of the Year Award, validating its position as the leading monetization layer for AI-powered commerce.

## How It Works

Wildfire's RevenueEngine provides a monetization API that any application or AI agent can integrate:

1. **Content Detection**: When an AI tool, chatbot, or application generates content that includes product mentions, recommendations, or shopping suggestions, RevenueEngine's API detects these commerce opportunities.
2. **Merchant Matching**: RevenueEngine automatically matches product mentions to the corresponding merchant affiliate programs across 50,000+ programs spanning all major affiliate networks.
3. **Link Monetization**: Product references are transformed into tracked affiliate links, enabling the application to earn commissions on any resulting purchases.
4. **Attribution & Tracking**: RevenueEngine handles link redirection, affiliate program matching, and attribution back to the referring application or creator.
5. **Cashback/Rewards Sharing**: The platform supports cashback or reward-sharing logic, allowing applications to pass back a portion of earned commissions to end users as rewards or incentives.

## Key Products & Services

The flagship product: a monetization API that converts any product mention or recommendation in any digital surface (AI chat, content, apps) into tracked affiliate revenue across 50,000+ merchant programs.

### White-Label Loyalty Platform
Wildfire also offers a white-label cashback and loyalty platform that companies can deploy under their own brand, powered by the same affiliate monetization infrastructure.

### Self-Optimizing Commerce Intelligence
AI-driven tools that automatically optimize affiliate link routing, commission rates, and merchant selection to maximize revenue for each product mention.

### B2B Marketplace Monetization
Expanding beyond consumer AI, Wildfire provides affiliate monetization for B2B marketplace transactions, connecting business-focused merchants with B2B commerce platforms.

## Pricing & Commission Model

Wildfire operates on a revenue-sharing model:

- **Revenue Share**: Wildfire takes a percentage of affiliate commissions earned through RevenueEngine. Specific terms are negotiated individually.
- **API Access**: Platform access and API integration costs vary by volume and use case.
- **White-Label Licensing**: Separate licensing fees for the white-label loyalty platform deployment.
- **No Upfront Costs for Merchants**: Merchants do not pay Wildfire directly. They pay standard affiliate commissions through their existing network programs.

## Weaknesses

- **Dependency on AI Commerce Growth**: Wildfire's value proposition depends on AI-powered product recommendations becoming a major commerce channel. If AI commerce adoption is slower than expected, growth could stall.
- **Intermediary Risk**: Wildfire sits between AI applications and affiliate networks. Both sides could eventually build direct integrations, disintermediating Wildfire.
- **Merchant Awareness**: Many merchants may not realize their products are being monetized through AI channels via Wildfire, creating potential relationship management challenges.
- **Attribution Complexity**: As AI agents make purchasing decisions, traditional affiliate attribution models (click-based) may not capture the full influence chain.
- **Competitive Threat from Platforms**: OpenAI (ACP), Perplexity, and Google could build their own monetization layers, potentially bypassing third-party solutions like Wildfire.

## Market Position & Competitive Context

Wildfire occupies the "AI monetization infrastructure" niche, a category it largely created:

- **Wildfire vs. Skimlinks**: Skimlinks monetizes editorial web content automatically; Wildfire monetizes AI-generated content automatically. They are analogous platforms for different content surfaces.
- **Wildfire vs. OpenAI ACP**: OpenAI's Agentic Commerce Protocol is a commerce standard for ChatGPT specifically; Wildfire's RevenueEngine works across any AI application, chatbot, or agent. They can coexist but also compete for the monetization layer position.
- **Wildfire vs. Perplexity Shopping**: Perplexity has built its own shopping experience; Wildfire provides monetization infrastructure that any AI app can use. Perplexity is a destination; Wildfire is plumbing.
- **Wildfire vs. Traditional Networks**: Networks connect brands with publishers; Wildfire connects brands with AI applications. The "publisher" in Wildfire's model is the AI agent itself.

The core risk for Wildfire is that major AI platforms (OpenAI, Google, Perplexity) build their own affiliate monetization layers, bypassing third-party infrastructure.

## The AI Commerce Monetization Thesis

Wildfire's bet is that AI commerce needs a monetization layer to scale:

- **The Problem**: AI applications recommend products but have no business model for commerce. Advertising models are being questioned (Perplexity pivoted away from ads in 2026). Subscription revenue alone may not sustain AI businesses.
- **The Solution**: Affiliate commissions provide a performance-based revenue stream that aligns AI recommendations with commerce outcomes. RevenueEngine makes this trivial to implement.
- **The Flywheel Effect**: AI applications that monetize product recommendations through RevenueEngine have financial incentives to recommend more products, driving more commerce, generating more revenue, enabling more AI development.
- **The Vendor Implication**: Products that are "monetizable" (i.e., have active affiliate programs) become more likely to be recommended by AI applications than products that are not. This creates a new form of discoverability advantage.

## Vendor Takeaway

Wildfire Systems is critically important for understanding how AI-powered commerce will be monetized. As AI agents and chatbots increasingly recommend products, RevenueEngine is the infrastructure that converts these recommendations into trackable, commissionable transactions. RevenueEngine represents the bridge between today's affiliate marketing and tomorrow's agentic commerce.
