# Impact.com — Network Profile

## Overview

Impact.com (formerly Impact Radius) is a partnership management platform founded in 2008 by Per Petersen, Lisa Riolo, Todd Crawford, and Roger Barnette — several of whom were founding team members at Commission Junction. Headquartered in Santa Barbara, California, Impact.com has grown into one of the most prominent affiliate and partnership platforms globally, distinguished by its "partnership cloud" approach that extends beyond traditional affiliate marketing to encompass influencer, B2B, mobile, and strategic partnerships.

The company rebranded from Impact Radius to Impact.com in 2020, reflecting its broadened vision. Impact.com has raised over $300 million in venture capital and private equity funding, with investors including Providence Strategic Growth and others. The company's last known valuation placed it in the multi-billion dollar range, and as of early 2026, it is on pace to surpass $270 million in annual recurring revenue (up approximately 20% year-over-year), while powering close to $120 billion in partner-referred gross merchandise value (GMV).

Impact.com distinguishes itself from traditional affiliate networks by positioning as a SaaS platform rather than a managed network. Brands use Impact's technology to build and manage their own partnership programs, rather than relying on the network to broker relationships. This gives advertisers more control over partner selection, commission structures, and data ownership.

## Commission Models

Impact.com provides the most flexible commissioning engine in the affiliate industry:

**Commission Types:**
- **CPS (Cost Per Sale):** Percentage of sale — the standard model.
- **CPL (Cost Per Lead):** Fixed fee per qualified lead or action.
- **CPA (Cost Per Action):** Flexible action-based commissioning (installs, signups, trials, subscriptions).
- **CPI (Cost Per Install):** Mobile app install commissions.
- **Hybrid Models:** Multiple commission events on a single partnership (e.g., commission for lead + bonus for sale conversion).
- **Recurring Commissions:** Ongoing payouts for subscription renewals.
- **Dynamic Commissions:** Rules-based commissioning that adjusts rates based on: customer type (new vs. returning), product category or SKU, order value thresholds, geographic region, device type, promotional codes used.
- **Performance Campaigns:** Brands can hire partners to create and post content as a placement or media buy, with flat-fee or performance-based compensation.

**Typical Rates by Vertical:**

| Vertical | Typical Commission |
|---|---|
| Retail / Ecommerce | 5-15% |
| DTC Brands | 10-25% (often higher for new customer acquisition) |
| Travel | 3-10% |
| Finance / Fintech | $20-$200 per lead |
| Technology / SaaS | 15-40% (often with recurring) |
| B2B | $50-$500 per qualified lead |
| Mobile Apps | $1-$15 per install |

**Cookie Duration:**
Impact uses both cookie and non-cookie tracking. When cookies are used, durations are set by individual brands, typically 30-90 days. Impact's cookieless tracking via digital fingerprinting ensures attribution even when cookies are blocked or deleted.

**Payment Terms:**
- Configurable payment schedules per brand program.
- Most brands pay on Net-30, Net-60, or Net-90 cycles.
- Minimum payout thresholds set by individual brands.
- Payment methods: Direct deposit, PayPal, wire transfer.
- Impact.com itself charges brands (not publishers) for platform access, starting at approximately $2,500/month for basic plans.

## Technology & Tracking

Impact.com's technology stack is widely regarded as the most modern and capable in the affiliate/partnership industry:

**Tracking Infrastructure:**
- **Universal Tracking Tag (UTT):** Single JavaScript tag captures all conversion events.
- **Server-to-Server (S2S):** Direct API-based tracking for maximum accuracy.
- **Cookieless Attribution:** Proprietary digital fingerprinting using non-PII data to attribute conversions when cookies are unavailable. This positions Impact well for the post-cookie era.
- **Cross-Device Tracking:** Deterministic and probabilistic matching across devices.
- **Mobile App Tracking:** SDK-based tracking for in-app conversions and installs.
- **Offline Conversion Import:** Support for importing call center, in-store, and other offline conversions.

**Attribution Models:**
- Last-click (default for most programs).
- First-click attribution option.
- Linear attribution.
- Custom multi-touch attribution rules.
- Full conversion path visibility for advertisers.

**Shopify Integration:**
Impact.com offers a no-code Shopify plugin that handles:
- Automatic conversion tracking installation.
- Product catalog sync.
- Discount code management and automatic sync to Shopify.
- Post-checkout referral program promotion.
- Order data reconciliation.

**Amazon Integration (New 2025):**
Brands can now manage Amazon affiliate relationships directly within Impact.com. All commissions, performance data, and reporting are unified in the same environment as other partnership types.

**API & Developer Tools:**
- Comprehensive REST APIs for brands and partners.
- Webhook-based event notifications.
- Product catalog APIs.
- Partner management APIs (discovery, contracting, commissioning).
- Reporting and analytics APIs.
- SDK for mobile app integration.

**Reporting & Analytics:**
- Real-time dashboards with customizable widgets.
- Transaction-level detail including full customer journey.
- Cross-channel attribution reporting.
- Cohort analysis and LTV tracking.
- Custom report builder with scheduling and export.
- Fraud detection reporting and alerts.

**AI & Automation:**
- AI-powered partner discovery and recommendation.
- Automated partner onboarding workflows.
- Rules-based commission optimization.
- Automated fraud detection with machine learning.
- Performance-based partner tiering and management.

**Deep Linking:**
Full deep link support with automatic link wrapping. Partners can link to any page on a brand's site. Mobile deep linking supported for app-installed users.

## Vendor Relevance

**Anker:**
Anker runs its own direct affiliate program (8% commission, 30-day cookie via anker.com). Impact.com's marketplace includes several competing consumer electronics brands. If Anker were to adopt a partnership management platform, Impact.com would be a strong candidate because:
- Impact's Amazon integration (launched 2025) would allow Anker to unify its Amazon affiliate tracking with direct-site affiliate tracking in a single platform.
- Impact's DTC brand expertise aligns with Anker's direct-to-consumer strategy.
- Impact's dynamic commissioning could enable Anker to offer higher rates for new customer acquisition vs. repeat purchases — a strategy common among DTC electronics brands.

**Priority Bicycles:**
Priority currently uses AvantLink (5% commission, 21-day cookie). Impact.com hosts competing bicycle brands (Jenson USA runs a 3% commission program via Impact Radius/AvantLink dual network). Impact's broader publisher base could offer Priority access to lifestyle and general retail publishers beyond AvantLink's outdoor-endemic audience. However, the platform's pricing ($2,500+/month) may be prohibitive for Priority's scale.

**Strategic Intelligence:**
Impact.com's $120 billion in partner-referred GMV and 20% growth rate make it a bellwether for the partnership economy. Monitoring Impact's product launches (especially Amazon integration and AI tools) provides leading indicators for affiliate industry direction.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Modern technology: Best-in-class tracking, attribution, and cookieless solutions.
- Partnership breadth: Manages affiliates, influencers, B2B, mobile, and strategic partnerships on one platform.
- Brand control: SaaS model gives advertisers full ownership of partner relationships and data.
- Shopify + Amazon integration: Native integrations with the two largest ecommerce platforms.
- Dynamic commissioning: Most flexible commission rules engine in the industry.
- Enterprise traction: Walmart, Target, Airbnb, Shopify — marquee enterprise clients.
- Growth trajectory: 20% YoY revenue growth indicates strong market demand.
- Unified creator/affiliate/advocate: Base versions of Creator and Advocate solutions included at no extra cost.

**Weaknesses:**
- Cost: Starting at $2,500/month, Impact is prohibitively expensive for small merchants. This is not a platform for bootstrapped DTC brands doing under $1M in revenue.
- Complexity: The platform's feature depth creates a learning curve. Smaller brands may not use 80% of available capabilities.
- Not a traditional network: Impact doesn't broker publisher-advertiser relationships the way CJ or Awin do. Brands must actively recruit and manage partners. This requires dedicated affiliate program management resources.
- Publisher discovery: While improving, Impact's partner marketplace is less mature than CJ's or Awin's for publisher-initiated discovery.
- Support variability: Enterprise clients get white-glove service; smaller brands report less responsive support.

- Shopify integration enhancements include automated discount code sync and post-checkout referral promotion.

**Funding & Valuation:**
Impact.com is widely expected to pursue an IPO within the next 1-2 years, given its scale ($270M+ ARR), growth rate, and market position. A public listing would make it the first pure-play partnership management platform on public markets.
