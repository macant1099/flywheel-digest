# ShareASale — Network Profile

## Overview

ShareASale was founded in 2000 by Brian Littleton in Chicago, Illinois, and grew to become one of the most popular affiliate networks in North America, particularly beloved by small and mid-size merchants for its accessibility, transparency, and low barrier to entry. The network was acquired by Awin (then Zanox/Affiliate Window) in 2017 for an undisclosed amount, marking Awin's strategic push into the US market.

For nearly eight years after the acquisition, ShareASale continued operating as a separate platform with its own interface, tracking system, and brand identity. However, in 2025, Awin announced the full migration of all ShareASale accounts to the unified Awin platform. As of October 6, 2025, the ShareASale platform officially closed, and all advertiser and publisher accounts were transitioned to Awin.

This profile documents ShareASale's legacy capabilities and the transition to Awin, as the ShareASale brand retains significant recognition in the affiliate industry and many programs still reference it in their marketing.

**Key Dates:**
- 2000: ShareASale founded by Brian Littleton
- 2017: Acquired by Awin Group
- March 2025: New merchants/affiliates launch exclusively on Awin

## Commission Models

ShareASale supported flexible commission structures that carried over to Awin:

**Commission Types:**
- **Pay-Per-Sale (CPS):** Percentage of sale amount — the most common model, typically 5-20%.
- **Pay-Per-Lead (CPL):** Flat fee per qualified action (signup, form submission, free trial start).
- **Recurring Commissions:** Ongoing payouts for subscription-based products, paying affiliates for each renewal period.
- **Two-Tier Commissions:** Some programs paid affiliates for referring other affiliates.
- **Hybrid Models:** Combinations of base commission + bonus structures.

**Typical Rates by Vertical (Historical):**

| Vertical | Typical Commission |
|---|---|
| Fashion & Apparel | 8-15% |
| Home & Garden | 5-12% |
| Technology / SaaS | 10-30% (often with recurring) |
| Health & Beauty | 8-15% |
| Food & Beverage | 5-10% |
| Finance | $10-$100 per lead |
| Arts & Crafts | 5-12% |

**Cookie Duration:**
Varied by merchant, typically 30-90 days. ShareASale was known for transparent cookie duration disclosure — publishers could see exact duration on each merchant's profile page before applying.

**Payment Terms:**
- Net-20 payment cycle (commissions finalized on the 20th of the following month).
- Minimum payout: $50 USD.
- Payment methods: Direct deposit (ACH), check, Payoneer, wire transfer.
- Single monthly payment cycle.

**Transparency Features (Legacy):**
One of ShareASale's most valued features was its merchant profile transparency. Publishers could view:
- Average commission rate
- Average sale amount
- EPC (earnings per click) — 7-day and 30-day averages
- Conversion rate
- Cookie duration
- Reversal rate (percentage of commissions that get reversed)
- Program age and approval rate

This transparency has been preserved and enhanced in the Awin platform.

## Technology & Tracking

**Pre-Migration ShareASale Technology:**
- JavaScript tracking tags on merchant conversion pages.
- Image pixel tracking (legacy fallback).
- Server-to-server (S2S) postback tracking.
- Real-time transaction reporting.
- Product datafeed system for catalog distribution.
- Custom link builder and deep link generator.
- Affiliate API for programmatic access.
- StoreFront widget builder for affiliates.
- Video commerce tools for product promotion.

**Post-Migration (Awin Platform):**
The transition to Awin brought significant technology upgrades:
- **Enhanced Attribution:** Awin's Attribution Manager allows customizable attribution rules beyond last-click, enabling better budget allocation.
- **Cookieless Tracking:** Awin's advanced cookieless solutions using Chrome Attribution Reporting API and browser-level deterministic matching.
- **In-App Tracking:** Social media attribution through in-app browser breakout technology.
- **Enhanced Conversion API:** Server-side transaction identification for improved accuracy.
- **AI-Powered Partner Discovery:** Awin's AI suggests potential partners matching specific campaign goals.
- **Automated Link Migration:** All existing ShareASale tracking links were automatically redirected to Awin equivalents — no publisher action required.

**API Access:**
- Awin Publisher API (reports, programs, transactions).
- Awin Advertiser API (partner management, commissioning, reporting).
- Product feed APIs for catalog integration.
- Conversion API for server-side tracking.

**Reporting:**
- Real-time performance dashboards.
- Transaction-level detail with full attribution chain.
- Cross-device reporting.
- Customizable date ranges and segmentation.
- Exportable data in multiple formats.

Anker did not historically maintain a ShareASale program, operating its own direct affiliate program (8% commission, 30-day cookie). Now that these programs are on Awin, the consolidated marketplace creates a larger competitive landscape for Anker to monitor. If Anker were to diversify its affiliate network presence, Awin (which now absorbs all ShareASale programs) would offer immediate access to 250,000+ publishers.

- ICAN Cycling (formerly on ShareASale, 5% commission, 30-day cookie)

- Various e-bike and cycling accessories brands

The Awin consolidation means these competing programs now sit alongside a much larger publisher base, potentially increasing competitive pressure on Priority's AvantLink-only distribution.

**Intelligence Value:**

## Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths (Legacy + Awin Transition):**
- Massive SMB merchant catalog: 16,550+ programs provided unmatched breadth for niche publishers.
- Transparency culture: EPC, conversion rate, and reversal rate disclosure set industry standards that Awin has preserved.
- Low barrier to entry: Small merchants could launch programs with minimal investment.
- Recurring commission support: Popular with SaaS and subscription brands.
- Community loyalty: ShareASale built deep relationships with the affiliate marketing community over 25 years.
- Awin integration benefits: Access to global Awin network (180 countries), AI tools, and enhanced tracking.

**Weaknesses:**
- Platform discontinuation: The forced migration disrupted some merchants and publishers, with reported confusion during the transition period.
- Loss of identity: ShareASale's distinct character and SMB-friendly culture may be diluted within Awin's broader enterprise-oriented platform.
- Migration friction: Some publishers reported broken links, temporary tracking gaps, or confusion about new dashboard navigation during the Q2-Q3 2025 transition.
- Increased costs: Awin's pricing structure may be higher than ShareASale's historically low-cost model for some small merchants.
- Publisher sentiment: Some long-time ShareASale publishers expressed frustration with the forced platform change, viewing it as prioritizing corporate consolidation over user experience.

### 2026-04-28 — Awin Commission Rate Policy: 20% Cap + 7-Day Notice Rule
**Source:** Awin Help Center (Published March 9, Updated April 1, 2026) | **Type:** Policy | **Relevance:** Medium

Awin has formally codified commission rate change restrictions in its advertiser help center:
- **Max 20% reduction** per rate change event.
- **Once per 30 days** maximum frequency.

**Implications for vendors:**
