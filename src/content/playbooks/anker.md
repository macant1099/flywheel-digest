## This Week: Close the Biggest Gaps vs Benchmarks

### 1. Audit Amazon Associates leakage from tier-1 publishers
- **Gap:** Many tier-1 partners (Wirecutter, WIRED, CNET, The Guardian, Popular Mechanics, BGR) likely route Anker links through Amazon Associates (3%) instead of Anker's direct Impact program (8%)
- **Action:** Pull Impact publisher report → identify which tier-1 partners are NOT enrolled → send targeted recruitment with commission differential pitch (8% vs Amazon's 3%)
- **Impact:** Recaptures DTC revenue + customer data from highest-traffic editorial pages
- **New intelligence:** The Guardian "The Filter US" named Anker as "Best budget USB charger" AND "Best desktop USB charging station" in a 22-product stress test (Apr 5, 2026). Links route through Skimlinks/Amazon. BGR published 5+ Anker articles in Q1 2026. Both are high-priority conversion targets.

### 2. Respond to Belkin rate compression
- **Competitor intelligence (2026-04-24):** Belkin cut base commission from 0.8% → 0.48% (-40%) and compressed top rate from ~4% → 3.84%. Anker's 8% flat now outpaces Belkin by 16.7x at the base level.
- **Action:** Use this in recruitment pitches to publishers currently carrying both Anker and Belkin: "Anker pays 8% vs Belkin's 0.48% — your revenue per click is 16x higher promoting Anker."
- **Impact:** Competitive wedge for converting shared publishers to prioritize Anker placement.

### 3. Create partner-specific promo codes for top 5 new publishers
- **Gap:** No documented affiliate-exclusive offer infrastructure (Exclusive Offer Capability: 2/4)
- **Action:** Generate unique Impact-tracked codes for newly confirmed warm leads: GUARDIAN15, BGR12, ANDROIDPOLICE10, TRAVELLEISURE15, SLICKDEALS15
- **Impact:** Incentivizes direct link placement; tracks per-publisher conversion; differentiates from Amazon

## This Month: Address Competitor Advantages

### 4. Counter EcoFlow's Home Upgrade Program + EcoCredits
- **Competitor intelligence (2026-04-23):** EcoFlow launched Home Upgrade Program (Mar 31, 2026) — a community/creator loyalty layer using EcoCredits redeemable for discounts or non-profit donations. This is a second-order affiliate play: creators embedded in EcoFlow's forum community are less likely to promote competing brands (Anker SOLIX).
- **Action:** Anker SOLIX must develop a community/creator loyalty layer to compete. Short-term: extend SuperChargers program to SOLIX sub-brand. Medium-term: design an Anker SOLIX Ambassador program with comparable incentives.
- **Goal:** Prevent creator lock-in by EcoFlow in the home energy/portable power segment.

### 5. Close the cookie gap vs UGREEN
- **Benchmark gap:** UGREEN offers 60-day cookies on some networks vs Anker's 30 days
- **Action:** Propose 45-day cookie on Impact for top-tier publishers; test impact on attribution rates
- **Goal:** Move Cookie Duration from 3/4 → 4/4

### 6. Accelerate payment cycle
- **Benchmark gap:** Anker's 45-day validation + 60-day payment = ~90-105 days. Best-in-class is net-30.
- **Action:** Negotiate with Impact to reduce validation to 30 days, payment to net-30 (total ~45-60 days)
- **Goal:** Move Payment Terms from 2/4 → 3/4

### 7. Activate Recurrent Ventures triple-play (9to5Toys + 9to5Mac + Android Police)
- **New intelligence:** Android Police (Recurrent Ventures, same parent as 9to5Toys and 9to5Mac) scored 24/25 with 5+ Anker articles in Q1 2026. One commercial conversation covers all three properties.
- **Action:** Pitch Recurrent Ventures centralized commerce team for a unified Anker direct partnership across all three properties with exclusive promo codes per brand.
- **Impact:** Three high-traffic tech publishers converted from Amazon to Anker direct simultaneously.

### 8. Launch Rakuten cashback for core Anker catalog
- **Evidence:** Rakuten cashback active for SOLIX (2.5%) but not core Anker charging products
- **Action:** Expand Rakuten relationship to full Anker catalog at 2-3% cashback
- **Goal:** Capture high-intent Rakuten shoppers (15M+ active members)
